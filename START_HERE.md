## Non-negotiable production rules

A plan does not become canon. A draft does not update memory. An audit does not rewrite story facts. Only approved chapter text may promote facts into continuity.

Repetition across plans, mini deltas, scene cards, loadouts, guards, drafts and audits does not raise authority. Every instruction keeps the obligation strength of the source that authorized it. Editorial targets and free execution choices must not be audited as hard canon merely because they were copied through several work files.

## Cross-chat reproducibility gate

A new chat, agent or Codex session must establish all of the following before changing chapter work:

- the authority order is loaded;
- the task matrix is loaded;
- the planning-obligation levels are loaded;
- the relevant workflow and exact templates are loaded;
- existing chapter artifacts are checked for accidental obligation strengthening;
- Level 1–3 controls are separated from Level 4 editorial targets and Level 5 free execution;
- an audit will rank actual damage rather than distance from one proposed staging.

A chat that has not completed this gate may inspect files, but it may not close planning, authorize prose, close an audit, approve a chapter or promote continuity.
