## Scene execution receipt

| Scene | Character | Immediate attention | Immediate want | Pressure behavior | Practical competence or restrictive boundary | Owned line, action, silence, refusal, position or object handling | Material consequence | Knowledge / spoiler limit | Prohibited reduction | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Rules:

- `Pressure behavior` must show what becomes shorter, rougher, stranger, more active, more avoidant or more mistaken under this exact pressure.
- `Practical competence` cannot be replaced by a voice adjective when the scene gives the character a natural action lane.
- `Material consequence` must change action, timing, information, object state, available choice, tactical line, relationship distance or scene outcome.
- A restrictive boundary must name the invalid result it blocks and the allowed replacement or omission.
- A correct line with no consequence may still fail as superficial coverage.

## Receipt validity

The receipt is invalid when:

- it lists only file paths, traits or voice labels;
- a character has no stable/current-state synthesis;
- a decision-relevant character has no productive or restrictive consequence;
- removal leaves the scene unchanged without intentional background classification;
- replacement is possible without explanation;
- practical competence is ignored despite a natural opening;
- later-canon knowledge or maturity leaks into the current phase;
- existing ties are replaced by reaction to Mielle;
- every present character receives a beat for coverage;
- a substantial revision lacks comparison and second-order reread;
- the analysis could fit an unrelated chapter unchanged.
