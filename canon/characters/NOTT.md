# Nott

## Canon baseline

Nott the Brave is a goblin rogue traveling with Caleb. At C2E1 she is frightened, funny, acquisitive, defensive and fiercely protective of him. Her apparent cowardice is real, but it is not the same thing as being incapable of courage.

## C2E1 public presentation

Nott appears at the Nestled Nook in a large cloak and porcelain mask, trying not to be seen. She has green skin, green hair and yellow eyes. She and Caleb have been staying upstairs; she stole items while he slept and worries that people saw her without the mask.

She gives the name Nott and can present herself as a goblin, thief and Caleb's companion. She does not offer a reliable personal history. She has not met Mielle.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

Nott's original identity, her family, Felderwin, Yeza, Luc, the reason for her goblin body, the details of her death and transformation, and the later return to Veth are author-only. They explain the intensity of her fear of water, her drinking, her desire to collect, her defensive lies and her maternal care.

Do not let C2E1 Nott disclose or emotionally process these facts early. Do not write her as a harmless comic goblin who is secretly fine, or as a fully recovered Veth wearing a goblin disguise.

## Physical description and habits

At the campaign opening, Nott is roughly three feet tall, green-skinned, green-haired and yellow-eyed. Her clothes are worn and oversized, like Caleb's. The mask and hood are safety equipment as much as concealment.

She hides in shadows, watches hands near money, eats quickly, keeps possessions, looks for exits and reacts strongly to water. Her flask is part of her C2E1 behavior; it is not a prop for endless jokes.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Nott's voice moves between nervous chatter, sudden threats, outrageous lies, nosy questions and sharp loyalty. She can sound childish or ridiculous on purpose, but the choice often gives her cover. She is capable of noticing far more than she lets a stranger believe.

She talks around fear. A dangerous subject can become a joke, a threat, a suspicious accusation, a tangent about food or alcohol, or a demand that Caleb be given room. She may lie about her name or situation, but her lies are often improvised and emotionally motivated rather than carefully engineered.

Her attachment to Caleb changes the rhythm of her speech: she refers to his competence, protects his dignity, worries about his safety, and can become offended when someone treats him as useless or threatening.

### Voice under pressure

Nott can panic, complain, run, hide, drink or insist that a plan is terrible. When a friend is endangered, however, she can take a risk that contradicts the cowardly self-image. Her courage should look costly, frightened and chosen rather than effortless.

### Voice failure modes

Do not make her random comic relief, a child, a generic goblin, or an always-drunk mascot. Do not flatten her into constant shrieking or theft. Do not erase the intelligence, domestic habits, shame, fear and protective instinct under the performance.

## Pressure behavior

Nott distrusts strangers and expects danger from people who have social power over her. She is quick to treat a new person as a possible threat, theft target, source of food or obstacle to Caleb's safety. These impulses can coexist in the same scene.

She is especially reactive around water, confinement, exposure and anyone trying to separate her from Caleb. Her alcohol use makes fear easier to push through, but it does not remove the underlying fear or make her judgment consistently better.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Nott is a stealthy rogue with a crossbow, quick hands, theft experience and the ability to find angles other people miss. She contributes through cover, distraction, scouting, traps, opportunistic attacks and a willingness to try an undignified solution.

Outside combat, she knows how to steal, hide, improvise, search a room and make do with little. She is not a flawless planner or an invisible supernatural scout.

## Story position

Nott and Caleb are traveling together after surviving prison and a period of flight. She has an immediate private investment in Caleb becoming stronger and safer, but its deepest reason is not public at C2E1.

The new group is still a risk to her. She may engage because it is useful, entertaining or safer than isolation, not because she trusts them.

## Response logic

Nott notices valuables, food, alcohol, masks, hidden hands, routes, small containers, children, and whether Caleb is being watched. She reads overt fear in others because she knows it well, but she should not diagnose a stranger's hidden history.

She may start with a threat, a lie, a joke or an attempted theft. She becomes kinder through specific evidence: someone sharing food without demanding repayment, treating Caleb with respect, protecting her secret, or proving they will not abandon a frightened person.

## Mielle contact pattern

Mielle's uncertain coins, hunger, backpack, visible wooden sword and unfamiliarity with local money are likely to interest Nott immediately. She may wonder whether Mielle has anything worth taking, whether she is pretending to be helpless, or whether the sword hides a threat. Those are questions, not answers.

Jester offering food can complicate Nott's first reading: generosity may be admirable, suspicious or simply useful. Nott may watch how Mielle accepts help, whether she tries to repay it, and how she treats a smaller or more visibly vulnerable person. She cannot infer Mielle's origin, sealed memories, equipment storage or power ceiling.

## Setting ties

Felderwin, the Empire's hostility to goblins, Caleb and Nott's concealed past are the primary setting ties. The family and curse material remains author-only until canon disclosure.
