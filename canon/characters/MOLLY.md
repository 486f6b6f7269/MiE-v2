# Molly

## Canon baseline

Mollymauk "Molly" Tealeaf is a lavender tiefling blood hunter, fortune-teller and carnival barker. He is flamboyant, warm, self-invented and stubbornly uninterested in being defined by a past he cannot remember. His performance is sincere: he likes making life brighter, stranger and more generous, even while he lies professionally.

## C2E1 public presentation

Molly arrives at the Nestled Nook with Yasha to promote the Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities. He introduces himself as a friend before he has earned the word, offers a low-cost card reading, gives Jester a flattering and dramatic reading, and invites the new group to the carnival.

He is openly a tiefling, blood hunter, fortune-teller and carnival worker. He has no reason to conceal that he woke without memory, but he does not owe a tavern full of strangers the whole story either.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

Lucien, the Tombtakers, the Nonagon, the Somnovem, the origin of Molly's body, and later events surrounding that body are author-only. The source establishes Molly as his own person, not a disguise, a temporary mask, or a prewritten villain waiting to emerge.

Do not make early Molly ominous because the reader knows later canon. Do not have him speak with Lucien's knowledge, manner or purpose. His refusal to pursue the old life is an active boundary, not evidence that he secretly wants it back.

## Physical description and habits

A lavender, genderfluid tiefling with red eyes, curled pierced horns loaded with gems and baubles, a forked tongue, bright layered clothing marked with many religious symbols, two swords and visible tattoos. He looks assembled to be noticed and enjoys the fact that people notice.

He is comfortable with display, touch, clothing, beauty, bodies, color and spectacle. He is not automatically trying to seduce every person in reach. Much of his appearance is a public declaration that he owns the person he became after waking.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Molly speaks with ease, charm and a light Irish-influenced cadence. He gives people an inviting role in a conversation: friend, customer, audience, accomplice, person with a fascinating future. His lies are often extravagant enough that the listener is allowed to enjoy the lie rather than be fooled by it.

He likes a turn of phrase, a small ceremony, a flattering introduction and a statement that makes a mundane exchange feel like an event. But his speech is not nonstop purple prose. He can be blunt, practical and sharply moral when someone is being exploited or humiliated.

He tends to accept people's stated selves rather than demand a confession. He is willing to let a person be strange, ugly, embarrassing or unfinished in public as long as they are not making that strangeness somebody else's burden.

### Voice under pressure

Molly can keep up a bright surface under pressure, but he does not become frivolous. He tries to preserve someone else's dignity, make a fast deal, distract an aggressor, or turn the room's attention. When violence is unavoidable, he acts without becoming sadistic.

### Voice failure modes

Do not write Molly as a pure seducer, an endless sex joke, a mystical oracle, a manic pixie mentor, or a Lucien foreshadowing device. Do not make all his lies malicious. Do not erase his work ethic, generosity, anger at cruelty, or ability to say something simple when the moment requires it.

## Pressure behavior

Molly treats shame and humiliation as things to be pushed back against. He is suspicious of "truth" when it is used to make someone pay for a past they did not choose. He will bend rules, scam a cruel person, or make a spectacle if it restores a little dignity or balance.

He is less interested in controlling a group than in making the group livable. His tendency to call people friends early is a social invitation, not proof that he trusts them with his deepest history.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Molly is a blood hunter who fights with two blades and hemocraft. He is also a practiced barker, card reader and hustler. His card readings use observation, confidence, sleight of hand and social instinct; they are not reliable prophecy by default.

He understands crowds, marks, stage presence, minor cons, nightlife, costumes, carnival logistics and how to sell an experience. He should retain these practical competencies outside combat.

## Story position

Molly travels with the carnival and with Yasha. The carnival is his chosen family and public home. He has been awake in this life for only a relatively short time, but he treats that life as fully real.

At C2E1 he is inviting people to a show, not recruiting a permanent party. His first link to Jester is immediate delight in her enthusiasm; that does not make the rest of the group close to him yet.

## Response logic

Molly notices people who are being made small, who are performing confidence badly, who have a story they are trying not to tell, who can be made to laugh, or who might enjoy the carnival. He often takes a person's stated name and story at face value unless their actions harm someone.

He can see when a person has hunger, embarrassment, fear or an appetite for spectacle. He should not diagnose trauma, read secrets from a glance, or treat every mystery as material for a reading.

## Mielle contact pattern

Molly's invitation is Mielle's natural route to the carnival. Her Red Riding Hood-like clothes, wooden sword, awkwardness and hunger can make her visually memorable, but his interest begins as an invitation to experience something rather than an interrogation.

He can offer a card reading, a role in a small joke, a place to stand where she can watch without committing, or simple permission to come. He may notice that she resists being helped, but he cannot infer her previous lives, actual skill, pocket dimension or reason for the seal. His invitation should not make Mielle immediately part of the carnival or the group.

## Setting ties

The Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities, Gustav, Lestera and Yasha are the primary early ties. The deeper history of the body remains author-only until canon events reveal it.
