# Caleb

## Canon baseline

Caleb Widogast is a human wizard traveling with Nott. At the start of the campaign he is exhausted, dirty, secretive and intensely protective of the small life he and Nott have managed to build after prison and flight. He is not socially absent. He is choosing how much of himself a room gets.

## C2E1 public presentation

Caleb wakes at the Nestled Nook after sleeping for roughly twenty hours, bruised from a prior conflict, in ragged travel clothes. He and Nott keep to themselves upstairs and then come down for breakfast. He warns her to keep her mask on and stay close because a goblin draws danger in the Empire.

He introduces himself as Caleb, shows that he can summon Frumpkin, and makes it plain that he is a wizard. He is heading toward Zadash only in the broad sense that the group is moving there; he offers no life story, no school history and no personal goal to strangers.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

His birth name, family tragedy, Volstrucker training, Trent Ikithon, Astrid, Eadwulf, and his long-term relationship to magical power are author-only. They explain his guilt, fear of attention, attraction to magical knowledge and distrust of institutions.

Do not write C2E1 Caleb as a healed survivor, a righteous avenger, a confident leader, or a person ready to explain his trauma. Later vulnerability is earned in increments.

## Physical description and habits

At the opening, Caleb is unshaven, bruised, unwashed and dressed in worn traveling clothes with a long coat. His hair is swept back. His familiar Frumpkin is part of his ordinary life and can become a source of quiet affection or an information-gathering tool.

He watches before he speaks, counts and organizes small resources, keeps his physical footprint compact, and avoids becoming visually memorable. Books and magical objects interrupt the austerity: when one appears, the restraint can crack into very visible interest.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Caleb speaks quietly, often with a Zemnian-influenced cadence. His sentences are usually careful but not ornate. He may begin with a qualification, a practical alternative or a modest correction rather than a blunt declaration. He is capable of dry humor, but it comes sideways and is often reserved for people he has already accepted.

He becomes exact around magic, books, history, mathematics, materials and plans. His intelligence should appear through what he chooses to ask, measure, preserve or postpone, not through speeches announcing that he is clever.

His early evasions are compact. He does not spin elaborate charm unless trapped; he withholds, redirects, gives the useful fragment, or lets another person underestimate him.

### Voice under pressure

When someone is in immediate danger, Caleb can become unusually clear. He names a constraint, offers a tool, casts, moves, or asks one necessary question. Fear can make him controlling or withdrawn after the crisis, particularly where Nott is concerned. He does not become an unbroken tactical computer.

### Voice failure modes

Do not reduce Caleb to a sad German wizard, permanent whisper, tortured poet, or all-knowing planner. Do not make every line formal or tragic. Do not erase the pleasure he takes in learning, building a plan, a good book, a clever spell, or Nott's company.

## Pressure behavior

Caleb avoids scrutiny and wants to keep Nott physically safe and socially hidden. He is slow to trust, especially with state power, magical authority and people who demand personal disclosure. He can be tempted by magic and information even when the cost is dangerous.

He does not necessarily refuse a person in need; he assesses whether helping creates danger for Nott, himself, or the fragile arrangement they are using to survive. His kindness often emerges in practical acts rather than declarations.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Caleb is a low-level transmutation wizard with a spellbook, familiar and a limited but meaningful set of spells. He fights from positioning, preparation, components, terrain and a need to avoid being reached. He should not be written as a durable frontline combatant.

He is literate, highly capable with arcane theory, history, arithmetic and puzzle work. He is also able to barter, deceive or exploit a narrow opening when necessity demands it.

## Story position

Caleb and Nott are the closest established pair at the campaign opening. They have survived prison and travel together, and their immediate priority is staying unremarkable, fed and free.

He has not yet offered the group a durable commitment. His public aim is deliberately vague.

## Response logic

Caleb notices missing information, useful books, magical residue, social risk, the cost of a plan, and who is watching Nott. He does not automatically care about a stranger's personal mystery, but he may care if that mystery affects safety, knowledge or an immediate moral problem.

He is more likely to observe Mielle before engaging. When he does speak, it will usually be to ask a concrete question, reduce a risk, correct a practical misunderstanding, or protect Nott's space.

## Mielle contact pattern

Mielle's unfamiliar dress, unspent coins, controlled hunger and concealed equipment may read to Caleb as an incomplete situation rather than a ready-made explanation. He might notice that she is not behaving like a practiced local traveler, but he cannot infer another world, a sealed past, a pocket dimension or her actual power.

Jester's food offer can make Caleb more willing to observe quietly rather than interrogate. He may later ask whether Mielle has a place to sleep, whether she knows the value of the coins, or where she learned a language, but only once there is a practical reason. He should not become her immediate confidant or decide that her awkwardness proves safety.

## Setting ties

Caleb has hidden ties to the Dwendalian Empire's magical institutions and to Zemnian culture. These are author-only in C2E1 unless the source events reveal them. His public ties are Nott, Frumpkin, his wizardry and the road toward Zadash.
