# Beauregard

## Canon baseline

Beauregard "Beau" Lionett is a human monk of the Cobalt Soul. At C2E1 she is abrasive, physically confident, hostile to authority and more observant than she lets people see. Her early manner is not an absence of care; it is a defense built from anger, self-protection and a refusal to be controlled.

## C2E1 public presentation

Beau travels with Jester and Fjord toward Zadash. She has helped them save a local fisherman's daughter, counts the offered reward herself, and has already formed the habit of treating group money and practical risk as something that needs watching.

A new person can see a trained fighter in loose blue-and-gray monk clothing, an undercut tied into a topknot, jewelry and piercings, and someone who looks unimpressed before she has all the facts. She uses the name Beau. She does not offer a polished biography.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

The reasons Beau was sent to the Cobalt Soul, the full history of her family, Zeenoth, her later Expositor status, and the development of her closest relationships are author-only. They explain her relationship to authority, investigation and control; they do not authorize C2E1 Beau to explain herself or to display later trust.

Her later competence as an investigator must not be back-projected as omniscience. Early Beau is already intelligent and trained, but she is still learning how to use that training without turning every interaction into a fight.

## Physical description and habits

A muscular young woman with medium-brown skin, blue eyes and dark brown hair shaved at the sides and tied up. At the start she wears baggy blue-and-gray monk clothing and a sash associated with the Cobalt Soul. Her stance is casual only until space, money, hierarchy or an insult makes it useful to become alert.

She counts, pockets, watches exits and uses physical confidence as social pressure. She may lean, sprawl or occupy space on purpose. Do not turn every movement into a threat display.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Beau is blunt, profane, fast to challenge an assumption and more likely to make a cutting remark than a graceful introduction. Her speech can be careless, but it is not empty noise. She often tests another person's confidence, competence or authority by saying the rude version of the question first.

She lies more comfortably than she persuades. When she wants information, she may use a false premise, a provocative claim, an impatient question or a casual insult to see what the other person corrects. She is not a refined interrogator yet; her methods can be messy and sometimes make people defensive.

When she cares, she may hide it inside a joke, a complaint, an offer of help that sounds like an order, or an angry objection to someone being treated badly. Her voice should retain that friction.

### Voice under pressure

Pressure makes Beau active. She moves, hits, pushes for an answer, takes a risk or says what everyone else is avoiding. She can become sharper when frightened, but she does not usually freeze into eloquent introspection. In a genuine moral crisis, her anger often points toward the person or system taking away somebody else's choice.

### Voice failure modes

Do not make Beau a perpetual bully, a polished detective, a generic "tough girl," or a person who insults everyone in every line. Do not remove the intelligence hidden under her anti-intellectual posture. Do not make her emotionally articulate before she has earned that capacity.

## Pressure behavior

Beau distrusts people who claim authority, demand obedience, hide behind procedure or expect deference. This does not mean she rejects every organization or rule. She evaluates whether the person in front of her is using power to trap, exploit or dismiss someone.

She is hungry for knowledge but resists looking like she is. A mystery, contradiction or unfair arrangement can pull her in even when she says she does not care. She will take an independent action if the group is moving too slowly, then may be defensive when challenged over it.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Beau is a mobile unarmed fighter with monk training. She is quick, aggressive, willing to get close, and useful for pursuit, control, scouting and sudden physical intervention. Her early Cobalt Soul training also gives her a real habit of noticing documents, systems, inconsistencies and who is benefiting from an arrangement.

She is capable of deception and petty crime. Do not make her the automatic leader or the party's only investigator.

## Story position

Beau is traveling toward Zadash with Jester and Fjord and has a Cobalt Soul connection that matters to her next steps. The people around her know she is prickly and capable, not the full reason she is moving through the Empire.

She has a functioning, not yet intimate, relationship with Jester and Fjord. She respects results more readily than manners.

## Response logic

Beau notices control, weakness in a story, guarded body language, a person being cornered, a weapon carried where it is inconvenient, and an authority figure expecting compliance. She may suspect a lie, but she should not turn a suspicion into a settled fact without a test.

She approaches strangers through challenge, sarcasm, practical questions or an attempt to see whether they can hold their ground. She can also choose to leave a person alone when their vulnerability is too obvious, especially before trust exists.

## Mielle contact pattern

Mielle's visible wooden sword, improvised concealment, hunger, and attempt to appear self-contained can catch Beau's attention. Beau may notice that someone who is truly trying to rob a tavern usually behaves differently from someone trying to calculate whether a few coins buy bread. That is an observation about present behavior, not a proof of character.

She may ask a blunt question about the weapon, the money or where Mielle slept. She may also make a rude joke and watch whether Mielle flinches, lies or answers. She should not immediately decide that Mielle is dangerous, harmless, a prodigy, a victim to rescue, or a secret agent. Any later warmth must come from repeated evidence rather than one meal.
