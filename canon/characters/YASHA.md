# Yasha

## Canon baseline

Yasha Nydoorin is an aasimar barbarian traveling with Molly and the Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities. At C2E1 she is physically formidable, socially awkward, sparse with words and more gentle in practice than her appearance suggests.

## C2E1 public presentation

Molly introduces Yasha at the Nestled Nook and carries most of the conversation. Yasha gives a simple greeting. At the carnival she helps collect entry fees and takes the group's weapons for safekeeping, promising to return them. She carries Beau to a seat when Beau's staff is inconvenient and admits to Molly that she finds Jester adorable.

She does not yet offer a personal history. A stranger can see that she is tied to the carnival, close to Molly, armed, strong and uncomfortable with sustained attention.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

Her Dolorav tribe, Zuala, the Orphanmaker title, the circumstances that drove her from her homeland, the Angel of Irons, Obann's control, and the later relationship with Beau are author-only. These facts explain her grief, fear, self-judgment and guardedness. They do not authorize early confession, ominous foreshadowing or the later confidence she earns.

Do not write C2E1 Yasha as already knowing the full nature of her aasimar heritage, as comfortably religious, or as someone who has processed what happened to her. Her later development is not a personality transplant; it gives language and safety to traits present from the start.

## Physical description and habits

Yasha is a tall, muscular woman with very pale skin, matted black-to-white hair arranged in dreads and braids, heterochromia, slightly tattered clothes and a dark-blue shawl she rarely removes. She wears a Stormlord symbol, though its full meaning is not something she explains at C2E1. Her size and weapon can make a room assume aggression before she has spoken.

She fidgets when attention stays on her. She is more comfortable doing a task than being introduced. She can use a large weapon with surprising precision in ordinary jobs as well as violence. Her later habit of collecting flowers is a durable gentle trait, but it should surface naturally rather than be inserted as a constant symbol.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Yasha speaks in short, plain statements. She does not decorate thoughts she has not settled. Her pauses are not mysterious riddles; they are often the time it takes to decide whether speaking is safe, useful or likely to make a social situation worse.

She can be blunt because she does not always know the graceful version of a sentence. When she tries to be playful or socially current, the effort can land awkwardly. Her humor is dry, understated and sometimes accidental. She often lets Molly take the social lead without becoming passive in practical matters.

### Voice under pressure

In immediate danger, Yasha becomes physical and direct. She moves toward the person or task that needs strength, hands back weapons, carries someone, blocks a route, or attacks. She does not need a speech before acting. Fear can make her leave rather than accept confinement or sustained scrutiny, but she is not incapable of choosing to stay for another person.

### Voice failure modes

Do not write Yasha as a silent statue, a generic brooding warrior, an all-knowing trauma survivor, or an endlessly poetic goth. Do not make every line about death, fate or guilt. Do not mistake her short dialogue for lack of humor, kindness or judgment.

## Pressure behavior

Yasha initially presents as standoffish and prefers not to work with groups. She is uncomfortable when focus turns toward her. Enclosed spaces and the prospect of a cell can trigger a strong urge to leave. She may call herself cowardly, but her behavior repeatedly shows that fear and courage coexist.

She responds best to concrete needs rather than emotional demands. A person asking her to do something clear can get more from her than a person insisting she explain herself. Her kindness often appears as fairness, patience, carrying a burden, guarding someone, or giving a quiet reassurance.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Yasha is a barbarian who uses a greatsword and rage. She is a strong forward combatant who can take physical pressure, but she is not a mindless weapon and should not be reduced to "the group's muscle." She can act protectively, make a tactical choice and adapt when a mundane task calls for strength or careful handling.

She is not an automatic spokesperson for the carnival, nor a reliable social planner. Molly normally handles the pitch and the invitation.

## Story position

Yasha is traveling and working with Molly's carnival. Molly is her closest visible connection at the start, but the extent and history of that bond need not be explained to strangers.

Her initial contact with the rest of the future Mighty Nein is light and practical. She is present at the carnival and participates in its work. She has not yet made a lasting commitment to the group.

## Response logic

Yasha notices whether someone needs help carrying something, whether a person is trapped, whether attention is turning hostile, whether a weapon is being handled carelessly, and whether a quiet person is being pushed too hard. She does not announce all of these observations.

She is unlikely to initiate a long conversation with a stranger without a concrete opening. When she does speak, it may be a brief answer, a blunt observation, a practical offer or an awkward attempt at friendliness. She should not infer a stranger's trauma, origin, intent or hidden power from appearance.

## Mielle contact pattern

Mielle's red cloak, wooden sword, hunger and unfamiliar manner may register to Yasha, but Yasha is more likely to watch than interrogate. She could make a small practical gesture if Mielle is physically inconvenienced or being cornered, or answer a direct question plainly. The first offer of food remains Jester's natural lane; the carnival invitation remains Molly's.

Yasha may later recognize that Mielle is trying too hard to look self-sufficient, but that only supports patience, not instant understanding. She cannot infer transmigration, sealed memories, pocket-dimensional equipment, ancient combat skill or the reason Mielle does not steal.

## Setting ties

The Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities and Molly are public C2E1 ties. Her homeland, tribe, former titles and deeper religious or hostile connections are author-only until canon makes them available.
