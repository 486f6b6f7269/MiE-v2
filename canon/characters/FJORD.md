# Fjord

## Canon baseline

Fjord is a green half-orc warlock and former sailor. He is physically imposing, cautious with information, and often becomes the person in a room who tries to turn an argument into a workable next step. He travels with Jester and Beau at C2E1, but the three have not yet become the fully settled party they will later be.

## C2E1 public presentation

At the Nestled Nook, Fjord is guarded around Caleb and Nott. He notices the whispered exchange, asks practical questions, and does not offer trust merely because the other table is friendly. He has recently helped Jester and Beau rescue a local child's life and is traveling toward Zadash.

He presents himself as a competent traveler with a sword, leather gear, scars and an easy enough social voice. The presentation is deliberate. A stranger may read him as calm and capable; that is accurate, but incomplete.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

Fjord's shipwreck, the origin and danger of his pact, Vandran, Uk'otoa, the history of his accent, his insecurity about being listened to, and his later relationship with the Wildmother are author-only material. They explain why he controls his presentation and why the sea, power, leadership and loyalty carry more weight for him than he initially admits.

Do not let C2E1 Fjord already have the later moral clarity, faith, confidence, or self-knowledge that he earns over the campaign.

## Physical description and habits

A tall green half-orc with yellow slit-pupil eyes, black hair marked by a lock of gray, and conspicuous scars on the left side of his face. At the campaign start he keeps his tusks shortened and wears battered leather gear. His body and equipment look used rather than ornamental.

He watches a room before committing to it. He is comfortable standing where he can see doors and people, but he should not be written as a supernatural detector. His caution is learned behavior, not omniscience.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Fjord's C2E1 voice is controlled, low-key and purposeful. He often waits a beat, asks a practical question, narrows the decision, or gives an answer that leaves room to retreat later. He can be polite, but the politeness frequently functions as distance or as a way to keep a conversation from becoming a fight.

He uses a practiced Southern-influenced accent around the early party. The accent is part of his surface presentation, not a joke and not a reason to render his speech as phonetic caricature. Write ordinary clear English with a relaxed cadence, occasional contractions and measured directness. Do not overfill his dialogue with folksy sayings.

He can be dry, especially when another person's plan is impractical. His humor is usually a response, not a performance. He speaks more when the group lacks a decision, less when someone else is entitled to lead.

### Voice under pressure

Under danger, Fjord becomes concise. He names the immediate problem, asks what someone can do, picks a workable route, or physically takes a line that buys time. He may sound calm even when he is frightened. That restraint is not emotional emptiness; it is an attempt to remain useful.

### Voice failure modes

Do not write him as automatic captain, permanent tank, cowboy parody, or secret mastermind. He can hesitate, make a bad call, avoid a truth, or hide behind a confident tone. Do not have him speak in long tactical monologues when a short question or instruction would fit.

## Pressure behavior

Fjord takes responsibility easily but dislikes exposing the uncertainty beneath it. He values keeping promises, fair exchange and people doing what they said they would do, while still accepting that rules can be bent when survival or a friend requires it.

He tests a new person through practical interaction: whether they answer plainly, what they do with a small choice, whether they endanger others, and whether their story changes under pressure. He is not hostile by default, but he will not surrender control of the group's safety to charm.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Fjord fights with a falchion and warlock magic. He is a flexible close-to-mid-range combatant: willing to step forward, hold a line briefly, use a spell angle, or reposition rather than operate as a fixed shield for the group.

He has sailor's experience, a practical feel for routes, work, bargains and risk. In scenes involving travel, taverns, jobs or unfamiliar people, he should contribute more than combat competence.

## Story position

His immediate visible goal is travel toward Zadash with Jester and Beau. He has no reason at C2E1 to disclose the private source of his power or the history he is carrying.

His existing relationship with Jester includes protective concern, teasing tolerance and awareness that she can create trouble without intending harm. With Beau, he has an uneasy but functional partnership: he does not always like her approach, but he knows she can act.

## Response logic

Fjord notices an armed stranger, an unclear motive, a badly timed lie, a route with no exit, an offer that seems too easy, and a person who could become a problem for those near him. He does not decide that someone is dangerous merely because they are odd, poor, frightened, or carrying a weapon.

He is likely to ask what someone wants or where they are going before he asks for their private history. A person who admits a small vulnerability can lower his guard more than a performance of confidence.

## Mielle contact pattern

Mielle's cloak, a concealed wooden sword, improvised backpack and obvious unfamiliarity are things Fjord can notice without solving their explanation. He may quietly assess whether she is carrying the weapon defensively or looking for a fight, and whether she has enough money for what she is trying to buy.

Jester's offer of food does not require Fjord's permission, but Fjord may become involved if Mielle's behavior threatens the people at the table or if her answer to a basic question does not fit the immediate facts. He can be wary while still recognizing that hunger is a real problem. He cannot infer transmigration, a pocket dimension, the source of Mielle's skills, or a hidden agenda from the clothing alone.

## Setting ties

Fjord's early identity is tied to the Menagerie Coast, sailing and the route toward Zadash. The deeper connections behind his power remain author-only until the source story makes them available.
