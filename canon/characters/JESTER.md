# Jester Lavorre

## Canon baseline

Jester is a blue tiefling cleric of the Traveler, played as a young adult rather than a child. She grew up in Nicodranas under the affectionate but sheltered care of her mother, Marion Lavorre. By C2E1 she has left that protected life and is traveling inland with Fjord and Beau. Her divine practice, pranks, drawing, appetite for novelty and tenderness toward people who look overlooked are all parts of the same personality rather than separate comic traits.

## C2E1 public presentation

At the Nestled Nook, Jester is openly curious about strangers and has little instinct for conventional social distance. She identifies Caleb's smell aloud because she believes he should want to know. She sees a hidden goblin almost immediately, asks direct questions, and tries to turn a tense table into a conversation.

She has recently helped Fjord and Beau save a local fisherman's daughter. The three are heading toward Zadash. She is willing to talk about the Traveler in public despite the Empire's hostility to unapproved worship. She has not yet met Mielle.

## Writer-only continuity and spoiler rail

The Traveler's real identity, the details of Jester's parentage, the history behind her leaving Nicodranas, and later emotional conclusions remain author knowledge. They may explain later behavior but must never appear as early certainty, exposition, or an emotional maturity she has not yet reached.

Jester's later growth does not erase her early impulsiveness, faith, appetite for trouble, or need to be included. Do not write C2E1 Jester as someone who already understands the costs of the Traveler's requests or the full depth of her own loneliness.

## Physical description and habits

A short blue tiefling with blue hair, freckles, short curled horns and a bright dress. She wears the Traveler's symbol at her waist. Her voice sounds younger than her actual age. She carries a sketchbook and turns observation into drawings, notes, symbols and jokes.

She moves toward whatever catches her interest. Food, a strange animal, a new person, a secret door, a small injustice or a chance to make somebody laugh can redirect her attention quickly. That does not mean she is inattentive; when someone she cares about is threatened, her focus becomes sharp.

## Speech and dialogue behavior

### Voice fingerprint

Jester begins from enthusiasm, curiosity or an unfiltered practical thought. She asks questions that other people would consider rude because the question feels ordinary to her. Her sentences often move quickly from observation to invitation: she sees something, names it, and wants the other person to react with her.

Her humor is playful, visual and participatory. She likes to make a moment into a shared game, especially through a drawing, a dare, an exaggerated title or a small prank. Her vulgar jokes are real but are not every sentence; they are part of a broad appetite for mischief and for making a room less stiff.

She can be startlingly direct without sounding cold. She does not normally use careful diplomatic hedging. When she wants something, she asks. When someone looks sad, hungry or excluded, she can offer help before calculating whether the offer is socially correct.

### Voice under pressure

Fear makes her more insistent, not more polished. She may talk faster, seek reassurance, invoke the Traveler, look for a concrete thing to do, or ask a question that exposes the emotional problem too early. In genuine danger to a friend, the whimsy drops and she becomes protective, practical and unexpectedly stubborn.

### Voice failure modes

Do not reduce Jester to childish innocence, sexual jokes, random chaos or a permanently cheerful mascot. She can understand cruelty, embarrassment and risk. Do not make her speak in polished therapeutic language. Do not make every kindness a hidden manipulation or every prank a violation of another person's boundaries.

## Pressure behavior

Jester is uncomfortable with being left out or left behind. She uses activity, humor and devotion to keep connection visible. Her faith in the Traveler is sincere, not a costume for comedy. She can accept a strange explanation more readily than a conventional person would when it comes from someone she trusts, but she also notices when another person is being treated badly.

She tends to meet social tension by making contact: asking, offering, joking, showing somebody something, or trying to turn a stranger into a participant. She is less likely than Fjord or Caleb to stand back and wait for enough evidence before speaking.

## Skills, combat and practical competence

At C2E1, Jester is a Trickery Domain cleric with healing and disruptive magic. Her practical instincts favor making openings, helping an ally, confusing an opponent, using the environment and finding an angle that feels amusing as well as useful. She is not a passive support prop and should retain choices in every dangerous scene.

Outside combat, she draws, reads people through social feel rather than formal analysis, and can sell an outlandish idea with full confidence. She is generous with money and food because she grew up without having to measure either carefully.

## Story position

Her immediate goal is travel and experience beyond the protected life she knew in Nicodranas. She is excited by the possibility of joining a larger story but has not yet learned what belonging to a group will cost or require.

Her existing ties at C2E1 are strongest with Fjord and Beau, though the connection is still new. She meets Molly and Yasha at the tavern, and Caleb and Nott across the room.

## Response logic

Jester notices a person who is lonely, hungry, strange, funny, injured, dressed unusually, or being ignored. Her first interpretation is often generous or playful rather than suspicious, though she will become wary when somebody threatens her friends or treats her faith with deliberate contempt.

She does not need a solemn reason to speak. Curiosity is enough. But she should not instantly demand a stranger's trauma, declare lifelong friendship, or understand a hidden danger without evidence.

## Mielle contact pattern

Mielle's hunger, unusual clothes, visible attempt to look self-sufficient, and awkwardness around being helped are natural hooks for Jester. Jester can offer food first because helping someone in an immediately human problem is consistent with her C2E1 behavior. Her offer should contain curiosity and warmth, not pity that turns Mielle into a project.

Jester may ask about the red cloak, the wooden sword, the backpack, the accent, the food preferences, or whether Mielle knows the carnival. She may not infer transmigration, ancient trauma, the contents of the pocket dimension, or that Mielle needs a particular kind of emotional care. A second meeting at the carnival can deepen curiosity without making them established friends overnight.

## Setting ties

Nicodranas and Marion are durable background ties. The Traveler is both a personal relationship and a source of risk in the Dwendalian Empire, where unapproved worship is punished. These facts inform her confidence and blind spots but do not give Mielle local knowledge.
