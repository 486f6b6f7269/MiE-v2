# Mielle invariant application test

## Purpose

This file prevents `canon/characters/MIELLE.md` from becoming a read receipt that does not materially affect planning or prose.

Reading the dossier, copying an invariant, mentioning a trait once, or writing a sentence that names the trait does not prove application.

The controlling question is:

> If this active invariant were removed, what concrete action, choice, limit, reaction, information boundary, object state, cost or scene outcome would change?

If the answer is `nothing`, `only tone`, `general atmosphere`, or an equivalent vague answer, the invariant is being used superficially.

## Two valid application modes

### Productive invariant

A productive invariant changes what happens on the page.

Examples:

- Mielle's desire for connection changes whether she answers a personal question.
- Her local ignorance makes her ask about money or law instead of supplying correct knowledge.
- Her clumsiness creates a small practical cost, another person's reaction, and a change in pacing or dialogue.
- Her preference against authority keeps her from taking command when she has the capability to do so.
- Her combat precision changes the route, timing or force of an action under threat.

A productive invariant must identify:

1. trigger in the scene;
2. concrete behavior or choice caused by it;
3. immediate consequence;
4. what would be different without it.

### Restrictive invariant

A restrictive invariant prevents a tempting but invalid result.

Examples:

- relationship boundaries prevent care from becoming declared trust, debt or party membership;
- local-knowledge limits prevent correct law, prices or geography from appearing without learning;
- Soulbond limits prevent recall from becoming duplication or remote attack;
- the closed left eye cannot become an emotion cue or proof of hidden power;
- system-name rules prevent internal mechanics from appearing in prose.

A restrictive invariant must identify:

1. the concrete temptation or likely draft error;
2. the result it blocks;
3. the allowed replacement, if the scene still needs the function;
4. where the audit will verify the absence or replacement.

`Nothing happened` is not enough. The blocked alternative must be named.

## Non-superficiality test

For every invariant declared active in a scene, complete one row:

| Active invariant | Productive or restrictive | Scene trigger or temptation | Concrete effect or blocked result | Evidence expected in prose | What changes if removed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

A row fails when:

- it merely repeats wording from `MIELLE.md`;
- the expected evidence is a label such as `Mielle was embarrassed`;
- the effect is optional texture that changes no decision, pacing, reaction or consequence;
- a restrictive row says only `do not break canon`;
- the same generic effect is copied into unrelated scenes;
- the draft could remove the evidence without changing any action, choice, limit, knowledge state, object state or consequence.

## Proportionality rule

Not every invariant must be active in every scene.

Do not force clumsiness, hunger, attraction, trauma, philosophical thought, a power reminder or a local-knowledge mistake into a scene merely to prove the dossier was read.

Mark an invariant inactive when the scene provides no natural trigger. The proof of serious use is correct selection and concrete consequence, not maximum coverage.

## Planning use

Scene cards select the active invariants and state their causal effect before prose.

The Chapter Loadout checks that:

- every active invariant has a productive or restrictive application row;
- no planned action contradicts an inactive but still binding hard boundary;
- each row names the point of use rather than only the source file;
- superficial rows return to planning before draft.

## Draft use

During the character pass, locate the planned evidence for every active invariant.

Do not add a sentence explaining the trait when the planned consequence is missing. Restore the action, choice, cost, reaction, limit or blocked alternative that the planning row required.

## Audit use

The audit applies the removal test:

> If the invariant and its evidence were removed, would the scene's action, choice, limit, reaction, information state, object state, pacing or outcome remain materially unchanged?

- `No`: application is material.
- `Yes`: application is superficial and requires revision or a return to planning.

The audit must also check proportionality. A scene fails if an invariant was forced into it without a natural trigger, even when the beat is visible.

## Relationship to authority

This file does not create new Mielle traits. `canon/characters/MIELLE.md`, current continuity, power files and approved chapters remain the sources for what is true.

This file governs how selected Mielle invariants must alter planning, prose and audit evidence.
