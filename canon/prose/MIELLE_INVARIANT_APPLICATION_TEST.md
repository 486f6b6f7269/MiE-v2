# Mielle invariant application test

## Purpose

This file prevents `canon/characters/MIELLE.md` from becoming a read receipt that does not affect planning or prose.

Read it with `workflow/03_PLANNING_OBLIGATION_LEVELS.md`.

Reading the dossier, copying an invariant, mentioning a trait once, or writing a sentence that names the trait does not prove a **result-bearing application**.

At the same time, not every natural trace of Mielle's personality, voice, body or habits must be promoted into an active material invariant. Incidental Level 4–5 texture may remain without a consequence receipt when planning does not claim it changes a result or blocks an invalid one.

## Classification gate

Before applying the removal test, classify the invariant use:

### Active result-bearing invariant

Planning expects the invariant to:

- change action, choice, pacing, reaction, knowledge, object state, cost or outcome; or
- block a named invalid result, leak, shortcut or relationship/mechanics implication.

An active result-bearing invariant receives a productive/restrictive consequence row and must pass the removal test.

### Binding background boundary

A stable rule remains true even when not foregrounded. Examples include knowledge limits, power limits, relationship non-results and system-name prohibitions.

It may pass through verified absence. Planning names the invalid alternative only when the scene creates a real temptation or risk.

### Incidental Level 4–5 texture

A natural trace of voice, awkwardness, attention, physical habit or preference that does not claim a material consequence.

Examples:

- one natural glasses adjustment;
- a minor awkward pause;
- Mielle using a preferred noun;
- a small dry observation;
- ordinary attraction affecting a glance without changing a decision.

Incidental texture is audited for voice, repetition, coherence and forced coverage. It does not need a material removal result merely to exist.

Do not retroactively declare incidental texture `active` only to justify existing prose.

## Controlling question for active result-bearing invariants

> If this planned active invariant were removed, what concrete action, choice, limit, reaction, information boundary, object state, cost or scene outcome would change?

If the answer is `nothing`, `only tone`, `general atmosphere`, or equivalent, either:

- the planned active application is superficial and must be revised or returned to planning; or
- the use was misclassified and belongs under incidental Level 4–5 texture instead.

Reclassification is valid only when no Level 1–3 result or protected boundary depended on the row.

## Two result-bearing application modes

### Productive invariant

A productive invariant changes what happens on the page.

Examples:

- desire for connection changes whether Mielle answers a personal question;
- local ignorance changes what she asks rather than allowing correct local knowledge;
- clumsiness creates a practical cost and changes another person's available action;
- aversion to imposed authority changes whether she takes command;
- combat precision changes route, timing or force under threat.

A productive row identifies:

1. authority and obligation level;
2. scene trigger;
3. behavior or choice caused;
4. immediate consequence;
5. what differs without it.

### Restrictive invariant

A restrictive invariant prevents a tempting but invalid result.

Examples:

- relationship boundaries prevent care from becoming declared trust, debt or party membership;
- local-knowledge limits prevent correct law, prices or geography without learning;
- Soulbond limits prevent recall from becoming duplication or remote attack;
- the closed left eye cannot become an emotion cue or proof of hidden power;
- system-name rules prevent mechanics labels in prose.

A restrictive row identifies:

1. authority and obligation level;
2. concrete temptation or likely error;
3. invalid result blocked;
4. allowed replacement or omission;
5. where the audit verifies it.

`Nothing happened` or `canon was not broken` is not sufficient when the invariant was declared active.

## Proportionality rule

Not every invariant must be active in every scene.

Do not force clumsiness, hunger, attraction, trauma, philosophical thought, power reminders or local-knowledge mistakes into a scene merely to prove the dossier was read.

Mark an invariant inactive when the scene provides no natural result-bearing trigger. A stable boundary remains binding without a staged reminder.

The proof of serious use is correct selection and consequence, not maximum coverage.

## Planning use

Scene cards:

- classify active result-bearing invariants separately from binding background boundaries and incidental texture;
- assign obligation levels;
- state causal effect or blocked result only for active result-bearing rows;
- do not turn Level 4 preferences into blockers.

The Chapter Loadout checks:

- every Level 1–3 active invariant has a valid consequence/boundary row;
- Level 4 active rows are genuinely intended to change the scene rather than merely decorate it;
- inactive hard boundaries are still respected;
- incidental texture was not promoted to satisfy paperwork;
- superficial rows return to planning or are honestly reclassified.

## Draft use

During the character pass, locate planned evidence for every active result-bearing invariant.

Do not add a sentence explaining a trait when the planned consequence is missing. Restore the action, choice, cost, reaction, limit or blocked alternative, or return to planning.

Allow incidental texture to remain natural. Do not amplify it into a second beat merely to prove application.

## Audit use

For active result-bearing invariants, ask:

> If the invariant and its evidence were removed, would the planned action, choice, limit, reaction, information state, object state, pacing or outcome remain materially unchanged?

- `No`: material application may pass.
- `Yes`: revise, return to planning or reclassify only when no Level 1–3 result depended on it.

For binding background boundaries, verify the named invalid alternative remains absent when the scene created a real risk.

For incidental texture, audit naturalness, voice, repetition and cumulative pattern rather than material necessity.

A scene fails when an invariant is forced without a natural trigger, even if visible.

## Relationship to authority

This file does not create new Mielle traits. `canon/characters/MIELLE.md`, current continuity, power files, user decisions and approved chapters remain the sources of truth.

This file governs how selected invariants are classified and how result-bearing applications are proven without turning natural character texture into bureaucracy.
