# Prose system

## Scope

This file governs prose execution for every chapter and every scene type: combat, aftermath, travel, investigation, social scenes, exposition, romance-adjacent material, humor, quiet time and transitions.

It does not decide canon, plot, power use, tactics, relationship state, continuity, source adaptation or chapter outcomes. Those remain with approved prose, continuity, Canon Delta, chapter plans, scene cards, power files, `canon/HOLMES_ENGINE.md`, `canon/COMBAT_RULES.md`, relationship state and user decisions.

This file controls how a permitted fact earns its place on the page.

## Core contract

Every material claim in prose must be supported by visible on-page evidence.

A material claim includes emotion, relationship, trust, fear, attraction, authority, competence, danger, intention, moral judgment, injury, exhaustion, power use, tactical advantage, inference, knowledge change, social obligation, legal/faction consequence and scene outcome.

A sentence may name an emotion, relation, conclusion or tactical state only after the scene has earned it through action, object, body, speech, omission, position, timing, cost or consequence.

A good prose fact can be touched, heard, counted, interrupted, paid for, withheld, moved around, refused, witnessed or carried into the next beat.

## Blocking rule: unsupported telling

Unsupported telling is a blocking audit failure.

A draft fails prose audit if it tells a material emotional, relational, tactical, inferential, power, injury, danger, authority, knowledge or consequence claim without prior or immediate on-page evidence.

Repair must add or restore evidence: action, object, body, speech, omission, timing, cost, position or consequence. Do not repair by adding more explanation.

Allowed telling is limited to:

- neutral compression between decisions;
- time passage with no material change;
- a previously established fact when the current scene is not using it as new evidence;
- a character's wrong self-description;
- a limited narrator naming a conclusion after the page has already shown the evidence.

Weak:

> Jester was worried about Mielle.

Usable:

> Jester looked at the bokken on the dirt, then at Mielle's empty hand. The joke she had been opening her mouth for did not arrive.

Usable after evidence:

> That was worry, or close enough to trouble me.

## Scene unit

Scene prose moves through six parts:

1. immediate condition;
2. pressure;
3. visible behavior;
4. choice;
5. consequence;
6. remainder.

A quiet scene still needs all six parts. The pressure may be money, a question not asked, an object between two people, a bad room, a legal order, a meal, a failed joke, fatigue, a route, a silence or a person standing too close.

A scene may vary the order under pressure, but the draft must be able to answer what condition begins the scene, what pressure makes it active, what behavior shows that pressure, who chooses, what changes and what remains.

## Description

Description should answer at least one question:

- What can be done here?
- What blocks movement or sight?
- What does someone notice because they care, fear, need or misunderstand?
- What object carries past action?
- What detail will matter if touched, moved, lost, broken, hidden, paid or witnessed?
- What detail creates the reader's picture with the fewest strokes?

Block:

- decorative inventories;
- abstract mood labels;
- physical traits used as character proof;
- lore paragraphs detached from decision or pressure;
- symbolic detail inserted before the scene has a body.

A detail earns space when it changes what someone can do, notice, risk, misunderstand, tolerate, choose or carry forward.

## Dialogue

Dialogue must come from immediate pressure.

A line should do at least one job:

- ask;
- refuse;
- deflect;
- test;
- conceal;
- offer;
- threaten;
- stall;
- joke to cover fear;
- correct a practical error;
- claim or reject authority;
- reveal more than intended;
- change the next action.

Block:

- therapist-clarity emotion summaries;
- theme statements;
- power-system exposition without pressure;
- unauthorized romance lines;
- equalized voices;
- dialogue tags that tell tone already carried by the line or attached action.

A character can reveal care by paying, staying, not asking, moving an object, leaving space or failing to make a joke. Dialogue does not have to carry the whole relationship.

## Holmes on the page

This section is prose-facing only. Full inference authority remains in `canon/HOLMES_ENGINE.md`.

Holmes prose must show:

1. observable clue;
2. selected meaning;
3. live alternative or uncertainty;
4. smallest action;
5. release of agency.

Block deduction that:

- states conclusion before clue;
- invents a hidden fact;
- reads a private thought;
- turns inference into moral certainty;
- removes another character's agency;
- uses elegant phrasing as a source of information.

Bad:

> He was lying.

Usable:

> He answered after the guard looked away. Too late for certainty, but late enough that I stopped watching his mouth and watched his hands instead.

Mielle may read pressure, mechanism, route, timing or contradiction. She may not turn one visible fact into a private biography unless the governing files and scene evidence support it.

## Combat on the page

This section is prose-facing only. Full combat authority remains in `canon/COMBAT_RULES.md`.

Combat prose must show:

1. board condition;
2. threat line;
3. perception or timing cue;
4. physical answer;
5. contact or miss;
6. cost or changed board.

Block:

- no-hit without cause;
- damage without mechanism;
- ally contribution reduced to background noise;
- Mielle stealing assigned results;
- power effect without capability/prohibition match;
- enemy threat not staged;
- adjectives such as `fast`, `precise`, `deadly`, `graceful` or `effortless` replacing line, timing, leverage, range, terrain, object or body cost.

Weak:

> Mielle dodged gracefully and broke its arm.

Usable:

> Its shoulder committed before the claw did. I gave the line one step, not two, and the arm spent itself across my body. The black edge came down while the limb was still open.

Combat remains ensemble prose. If another character owns a kill, rescue, spell, legal result, source action or finalization, the draft must stage their action instead of turning it into Mielle's narration.

## Backstory, lore and research

Backstory, lore and research enter only when front-story pressure touches them through:

- object;
- wound;
- debt;
- name;
- accent;
- failed assumption;
- law;
- price;
- spell;
- fear response;
- public contradiction;
- direct question.

Block:

- lore inserted because the author knows it;
- Exandria explanation Mielle has not learned;
- power explanation to reassure the reader;
- inherited archive memory used as free local knowledge;
- long relationship explanation before relationship changes behavior.

For crossover mechanics, planning files may name systems. Story prose should show output, limit, cost, witness reaction or consequence.

## Theme and symbolism

Theme is revision discovery, not drafting command.

A chapter or scene cannot end by telling the reader its meaning. It can end on:

- object;
- unanswered question;
- physical position;
- changed custody;
- silence;
- interrupted gesture;
- visible cost;
- someone choosing not to ask;
- a line that fails to solve the problem;
- a task that remains.

Do not make a paragraph announce what the scene means when the scene can leave the altered condition in the reader's hands.

## Revision order

Use this order after the factual scene exists:

1. **Story/source/continuity pass:** source order, approved outcomes, agency, object custody, knowledge, relationship limits and continuity.
2. **Evidence pass:** every material claim has visible proof on the page; unsupported telling blocks the draft.
3. **Voice pass:** Mielle and canon cast speak from immediate pressure, not from one polished voice.
4. **Cut pass:** remove explanation, repetition, soft adverbs, decorative lore, moral closing and unsupported telling without losing story, voice or evidence.

Use second-draft cutting as pressure, not a math law. Ask whether the draft can lose unnecessary weight while keeping the same story, flavor, clues, rhythm and character ownership.

## Stop condition

Stop revising prose when:

- material claims have evidence;
- scene pressure is visible;
- Holmes and combat claims obey their authority files;
- each speaker's line belongs to that speaker;
- the ending leaves a concrete remainder;
- further edits would replace one visible habit with another.
