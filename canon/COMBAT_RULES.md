# Combat rules

## Purpose

Combat is a fluid conflict of position, pressure, choice and consequence. It is not a transcription of RPG turns.

This file retains the useful parts of the former Combat Standard: readable geography, physical causality, opponent adaptation, minimal force, clear escalation and ensemble agency. It does not import later-story formations, fixed protection assignments, power tiers or visibility limits.

## Source adaptation

The transcript may establish who acted, what changed and what the result cost. Dice, initiative, repeated attacks and map-square movement are table artifacts unless a later consequence depends on them.

## Before prose: combat translation card

Every meaningful fight must establish:

- source obligations and character agency that must survive;
- material board: terrain, positions, exits, civilians and lines of sight;
- each side’s immediate objective;
- enemy combat language or repeatable method;
- the tactical truth that can decide the conflict;
- pressure waves and what changes between them;
- Mielle’s actual read, uncertainty and smallest response;
- independent actions belonging to allies;
- escalation trigger and aftermath.

## Pressure-wave cycle

Ground → Line → Read → Answer → Reply → Choice → Remainder.

- Ground: make the space usable to the reader.
- Line: identify the immediate danger.
- Read: use observable clues to build a bounded combat hypothesis.
- Answer: make the smallest effective correction.
- Reply: let the enemy, terrain or situation change in response.
- Choice: decide what must not be allowed to happen and what each person is willing to risk or reveal.
- Remainder: leave a physical and human consequence.

## Mielle in combat

Outside combat, Mielle is soft, awkward and often clumsy. In danger, she becomes exact and economical. She favors the least movement and force needed. Her restraint must read as a choice supported by conditions, not as artificial helplessness.

She may be frighteningly accurate when evidence supports it. She may not know hidden motives, unseen variables or arbitrary future choices. Her competence does not remove fatigue, pain, fear, moral uncertainty or allies’ agency.

## Ensemble rule

Every active ally must retain a meaningful lane: a decision, spell, opening, rescue, attack, retreat, warning or failure that belongs to them. No opponent exists merely to certify Mielle’s ability.

## Escalation

Escalation requires a changed fact: a new threat vector, a civilian, a revealed mechanism, terrain failure, an ally’s exposure, an enemy adaptation or another condition that makes the earlier answer insufficient. Do not escalate for volume alone.

## Aftermath

End on a remainder: an altered object, injury, missed chance, changed relationship, unanswered question, evidence left behind, route changed or decision owed. Do not end on a pose.
