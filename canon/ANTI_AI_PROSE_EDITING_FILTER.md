# Anti-AI prose editing filter

## Purpose

This is an editorial diagnostic filter, not a style generator, canon source or detector. It may improve existing prose, but it must not add story events, facts, characterization, stakes, POV shifts, dialogue purpose or canon.

## Before drafting

Check the plan for generic thematic framing, report-shaped prose, decorative abstraction, flattened dialogue, symmetry for its own sake and manufactured emotional closure.

## After drafting

After continuity, source, agency and mechanics checks, make only the smallest edits needed to correct clustered Critical or Important symptoms. Preserve facts, agency, voice, POV and emotional truth.

## Craft rules

- Begin with the event, observation, choice or consequence.
- Use specific actions, objects, constraints, names and physical details when they matter.
- Separate fact, inference and uncertainty.
- Do not replace a fact, motive, cost or consequence with generic language about importance or impact.
- Do not manufacture contrast, balanced phrasing or groups of three.
- Do not close every paragraph with a moral, implication or flourish.
- Show emotion through action, thought, sensation, omission, dialogue, timing and consequence.
- Keep narration inside the chosen POV.
- Give each character a voice shaped by their knowledge, mood, education and social position.
- Planning dialogue establishes intent and boundaries; it is never chapter-ready dialogue.
- In action, preserve the visible read, constraint, choice, result and cost.

## Hard limits

- Do not create a scene, reveal, relationship change or canon fact.
- Do not call text AI-written.
- Do not treat one isolated phrase as a problem by itself.
- Do not replace character voice with generic literary prose.
- Do not use this filter to bypass source fidelity, continuity, agency or power mechanics.

## Lexical and template check

Avoid generic praise, corporate phrasing, abstract metaphors, vague authorities and canned transitions unless a precise literal use requires them. Remove a sentence when it could fit an unrelated story unchanged.

## Finish check

Before delivery, ask:

1. Does each paragraph contain a specific action, claim, image or consequence?
2. Did the prose show what changed instead of announcing importance?
3. Did a character act from what they know and want now?
4. Did smoother phrasing make the text less exact?
5. Did any edit add information not already in the draft?

Stop when prose is specific, causally grounded and character-owned. Do not edit until every line sounds ornamental.
