# Locations

## Scope and usage

This is a campaign-wide location guide. It records enduring geography, social texture, jurisdiction, and practical writing consequences for places that matter across Campaign 2.

It does not establish a chapter's exact weather, room layout, crowd, travel time, patrol position, or scene choreography. Those belong to source extraction, episode mapping, scene cards, and approved continuity.

A location can be known to the author but unknown to Mielle. Do not use this file to give her a route, custom, map, rumor, or local relationship before she has encountered it.

## 1. Opening region: Marrow Valley and Trostenwald

### Marrow Valley

The Marrow Valley is a major Imperial region of farms, river and road settlements, former Julous Dominion territory, military movement, trade, and uneven Crown control. It is the right baseline for early Empire scenes: people work, travel, drink, bargain, serve institutions, fear institutions, and do not necessarily understand the wider political machinery shaping them.

### Amber Road

The Amber Road is the Empire's primary north-south arterial road, running between the Wuyun Gates and Rexxentrum through Zadash, Alfield, and Trostenwald. It carries merchants, soldiers, couriers, migrants, laborers, caravans, and people trying to leave one life for another.

Do not make it an empty safe road or automatic bandit territory. Its condition depends on season, local patrols, traffic, current conflict, and the episode source.

### Trostenwald

Trostenwald is a rural trade town in the southern Marrow Valley, east of the Amber Road and west of the Ustaloch. In 835 PD it has roughly 8,900 people and three wards: Lochward, Hillward, and Northward. It is governed by a Crown-appointed starosta, Fryda Langer, and guarded by a Crownsguard contingent.

Its economy is based on fish, crops, and sweet local ale called trost, made from a local grain and wheat strain. Brewery families Baumbauch, Husseldorf, and von Brandt remain prominent after an earlier brewing boom.

Trostenwald is the opening point of the campaign, but it is not merely an inn, a carnival, and a crime scene. It is a working road town with docks, market activity, brewing, fields, local households, guards, and people whose lives continue after the party leaves.

### The Nestled Nook

The Nestled Nook is a two-story Trostenwald inn providing food and lodging. Yorda runs it; Adelaine works as a barmaid. It has three upstairs rooms, a communal washroom at the end of the upper hall, an upper hall without windows, room windows, and a job board.

Do not infer a complete floor plan from these facts. The C2E1 extraction decides table positions, visible exits, crowd composition, food, price, and movement in the opening scene.

### C2E1 carnival grounds

The Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities is set up east of Lochward on the south bank of the Ustaloch at campaign start. The temporary arrangement of its tents, performers, animals, carts, audience, entrance, and work areas belongs to episode work.

## 2. Imperial cities and interior routes

### Zadash

Zadash is a large Imperial city in the Marrow Valley and the former capital of the Julous Dominion. It is built around the Tri-Spires and divided into the Outersteads, Pentamarket, Innerstead Sprawl, Tri-Spire, and Signet Wall.

It is useful because its social geography is physical: poor outer districts, market commerce, middle-class connective neighborhoods, elite towers, state offices, criminal networks, schools, archives, temples, prisons, and guarded gates coexist in the same city.

Important stable anchors:

- **Pentamarket:** broad everyday market and merchant activity.
- **Innerstead Sprawl:** connecting residential and institutional area; includes the Valley Archive of the Cobalt Soul and King's Hall.
- **Outersteads:** poorer outskirts and slum-adjacent communities beyond the main walls.
- **Tri-Spires:** wealthy, guarded elite district; includes high-status homes, magical education, entertainment, and civic power.
- **Valley Archive:** Cobalt Soul archive and temple of Ioun; public but monitored, with restricted holdings and training facilities.

Zadash scenes should preserve class, access, paperwork, gatekeeping, surveillance, and the fact that a different district changes what people can buy, say, wear, or get away with.

### Alfield and Felderwin

Alfield is an agricultural and mining town on the route between Trostenwald and Zadash. Felderwin is a larger farming settlement with local industry and a place in the Empire's food economy.

Use both as ordinary working communities first. Their later story relevance does not make every early resident suspicious or every field a clue.

### Hupperdook

Hupperdook is an industrial Imperial city marked by workshops, invention, factory labor, nightlife, smoke, machinery, and a culture where technical knowledge and labor meet military demand. It is not simply a steampunk set piece; industrial work, class, municipal authority, and fatigue should remain present.

### Rexxentrum

Rexxentrum is the Imperial capital in the Zemni Fields. It is a large administrative and magical center, home to the Crown, Royal Council, Cerberus Assembly, major military and scholarly institutions, and layers of court, bureaucracy, wealth, and surveillance.

Use it as a place where titles, access, papers, official audiences, institutional memory, and political consequence become materially heavier. Do not reduce it to a generic royal city or reveal later Assembly information early.

### Other Imperial anchors

Bladegarden, Kamordah, Deastok, Nogvurot, Pride's Call, and the Ashguard Garrison are lasting Imperial locations. Build a detailed sub-file only when the campaign reaches one of them or an approved chapter makes it necessary.

## 3. Menagerie Coast and maritime world

### Nicodranas

Nicodranas is a warm Menagerie Coast city under the Clovis Concord. It is a port, resort, trading center, and social world shaped by harbor traffic, visitors, local wealth, performers, servants, guilds, and private households.

The city can hold leisure and danger at once. Its sunlight, markets, beaches, and hospitality do not make it politically empty or socially equal.

### Port Damali

Port Damali is the largest Clovis Concord city and a major naval, commercial, and administrative port. Its harbor, merchant activity, ships, guilds, marines, customs, and elite homes make it a place where trade and state power meet.

Sea travel should involve weather, crew competence, charts, port law, cargo, reputation, money, and the possibility that someone else owns the ship or controls the route.

### Darktow and the Revelry

Darktow is a pirate center associated with the Revelry. Its rules, hierarchy, and culture differ from the Concord's but are not lawless in the sense of having no enforcement. Power there is personal, maritime, transactional, and backed by crew allegiance.

### Rumblecusp and the islands

Rumblecusp is a tropical island in the Lucidian Ocean. The broader Swavain island world carries maritime isolation, local belief, trade routes, storms, piracy, ruins, and communities that do not exist merely to supply a sea adventure.

## 4. Xhorhas and the Kryn Dynasty

### Asarius

Asarius, the City of Beasts, is a rougher Kryn settlement where goblinkin, orcs, drow, and other peoples live under Dynasty rule. It is a military and commercial town close enough to frontier danger that practical survival and local reputation matter.

### Rosohna

Rosohna, formerly Ghor Dranas, is the Kryn Dynasty's capital. Its urban structure, ritual life, military presence, and cultural mix make it a political center unlike Imperial cities.

The city should not be written through Imperial propaganda. Its darkness, architecture, Luxon faith, and population are ordinary to its residents. A visitor's confusion is not a neutral description.

### Bazzoxan, Urzin, and frontier Xhorhas

Bazzoxan sits near a dangerous historical boundary in the Penumbra Range. Urzin is a mobile settlement built around horizonback tortoises. Both show that Xhorhas is not one city or one battlefield; its people adapt to terrain, old wounds, religion, travel, and threat in different ways.

## 5. The north, wilderness, and old ruins

### Shadycreek Run and the Greying Wildlands

Shadycreek Run is a northern frontier town where formal Imperial law has weak reach and family, criminal, commercial, and personal power overlap. The surrounding Greying Wildlands are cold, sparsely settled, and dangerous, but not empty.

### Uthodurn

Uthodurn is a northern city with close dwarven and elven cultural presence, underground and surface life, craft traditions, trade, and strong local identity. It should feel neither like an Imperial town nor an undifferentiated fantasy dwarf city.

### Savalirwood and Molaesmyr

The Savalirwood is a damaged ancient forest. Molaesmyr, an elven city ruined long before the campaign, remains important as a source of history, grief, danger, and contaminated remnants. The forest's condition has causes that should emerge through the relevant arc rather than be narrated early.

### Eiselcross and Aeor

Eiselcross is a harsh northern land of ice, isolation, expeditions, dangerous weather, scarce safe shelter, and Age of Arcanum remains. Aeor is a fallen flying city whose ruins carry ancient magical science, lost systems, and lethal unknowns.

Do not write Eiselcross as a normal dungeon with snow added. Cold, supply, distance, visibility, exhaustion, research rivalry, and the risk of waking or activating old systems all matter.

## 6. Location use rules

- A location is not a mood board. Identify work, law, transport, food, language, climate, class, and physical limits that alter the scene.
- Keep exact staging in episode work. A stable map fact does not decide where a character stands.
- Do not use later destruction, political changes, or revealed secrets as early atmosphere.
- When the story first reaches a major place, create a focused episode setting addendum in `work/` rather than bloating this file with one-arc staging.
