# Factions and NPCs

## Scope and spoiler handling

This is an author-facing guide to institutions, recurring networks, and durable NPC anchors across Campaign 2.

It is not a character-knowledge ledger. A faction can exist without a C2E1 character knowing its purpose, personnel, secrets, or current operation. Every scene must still check what the POV and present characters can reasonably know.

Use the labels below as internal handling rules:

- **Public at C2E1:** can be used as ordinary setting knowledge when a character has a plausible reason to know it.
- **Known later:** exists at C2E1 but requires a later arc, contact, investigation, or disclosure before it enters scene knowledge.
- **Author-only secret:** may guide continuity and prevent contradiction, but must not leak into early narration, deduction, dialogue, or first impressions.

## 1. Dwendalian institutions

### Dwendalian Empire — Public at C2E1

The Empire governs Western Wynandir under King Bertrand Dwendal. It is a political system of Crown authority, local starostas, taxes, courts, regulated worship, military force, nobles, merchants, bureaucrats, and regional differences.

Do not make every Imperial worker personally loyal to every act of the Crown, and do not make every dissenter immediately revolutionary. People can comply, resent, exploit, fear, ignore, serve, or depend on the same system for different reasons.

### Crownsguard — Public at C2E1

The Crownsguard is the Empire's paramilitary gendarmerie, ordinary law enforcement, military reserve, and internal-security arm. Their presence changes the cost of public disorder, theft, violence, unapproved worship, and visible magic.

Standard equipment includes vermilion or maroon robes, bronze scale components, shield, helmet, crossbow, and basic longsword. Do not treat uniforms as a substitute for individual competence, ethics, knowledge, or authority.

### Righteous Brand — Public at C2E1

The Righteous Brand is the Empire's standing military and border-security force. It is more likely than the Crownsguard to define a scene involving war, garrisons, major routes, formal military movement, or the frontier.

### Cerberus Assembly — Public institution, deeper operations known later

The Cerberus Assembly is a small body of powerful mages centered in Rexxentrum and integrated into Imperial politics. Publicly, it is associated with elite magic, education, research, state service, and the Crown's magical authority.

Author background: the Assembly seeks magical relics and political influence, is institutionally hostile to the Cobalt Soul, and contains internal rivalries, secrets, and abuses. Do not reveal individual wrongdoing, hidden programs, or personal ties before the story reaches them.

### Soltryce Academy — Public at C2E1

The Soltryce Academy is the Empire's major magical school. It represents formal arcane education, social access, institutional discipline, and an entry point into the Assembly's world. It is not a neutral or universal measure of magical worth.

### Augen Trust — Known later

The Augen Trust is the Crown's intelligence network, based in Rexxentrum. It gathers information about threats to the Crown and competes with both the Cobalt Soul and the Volstrucker network.

Do not mention it before the campaign gives a character a plausible path to the information.

### Volstrucker — Author-only secret until disclosure

The Volstrucker are an Imperial mage-assassin network tied to the Assembly. Their existence, recruitment, methods, personnel, and connection to Caleb's past are not public C2E1 knowledge.

This entry exists to prevent accidental contradiction. It must not make a stranger suspicious of Caleb, turn the Empire into instant spy fiction, or alter early dialogue.

## 2. Knowledge, faith, and countervailing institutions

### Cobalt Soul — Public at C2E1

The Cobalt Soul is a scholarly religious order of Ioun. It preserves, verifies, studies, and shares knowledge through archives, researchers, monks, clerics, mages, and field agents. It has roots in the post-Calamity world and retained a place in the Empire after the Empire conquered the Julous Dominion.

Its Valley Archive in Zadash is public but monitored. Archives have access rules, escorts, restricted holdings, training spaces, and institutional priorities. They are not automatic libraries of every answer.

Author background: the Cobalt Soul and Cerberus Assembly have a long rivalry; the Soul may preserve dangerous knowledge rather than make it freely available. Beau's C2E1 rank, assignment, and personal history remain controlled by her dossier and later source events.

### The Traveler — Publicly obscure; Jester-specific at C2E1

The Traveler is the figure Jester worships. His symbol is an arched doorway over a road disappearing into the distance. His cult is unapproved in the Empire.

Author-only until the relevant arc: the Traveler's identity, nature, other followers, and later religious events. Jester's belief is sincere and must not be written as naïveté or fraud before the source supports a change in her understanding.

### Luxon faith — Known later for the party; public within Dynasty society

Luxon faith structures Kryn Dynasty society, including its religious language, social status, and practices around consecution. It is a real political and cultural system, not an exotic riddle for Imperial visitors.

At C2E1, the early party has no usable knowledge of its internal theology. Use Imperial rumor only as rumor, not neutral setting narration.

## 3. Trade, crime, and maritime networks

### Clovis Concord — Public at C2E1

The Clovis Concord is a confederation of Menagerie Coast city-states organized around trade and mutual defense. Its power appears through harbors, ships, merchant families, guilds, customs, marines, contracts, and port administration.

It is less centralized than the Empire, not less political. A coastal city can be open, wealthy, hospitable, exploitative, cosmopolitan, guarded, and class-stratified at the same time.

### Revelry — Public maritime reputation; local details known later

The Revelry is a pirate alliance centered on Darktow and maritime havens. It formed in opposition to the Concord's trade power and functions through crews, captains, personal reputation, piracy, protection, and island jurisdiction.

Do not write it as a single organized navy or a carefree pirate aesthetic. Its people have internal rules, rivalries, debts, and enforcement.

### Myriad — Known later

The Myriad is a broad criminal network whose cells operate through local cover identities, labor, contraband, coercion, and information. Its reach does not make every criminal in the Empire a Myriad member.

Its full structure and presence in a location must be discovered in-story. Do not use the name as early omniscient shorthand for crime.

### The Gentleman and local criminal operations — Known later

The Gentleman is a local crime figure in Zadash whose organization becomes relevant once the party reaches that city. His network is not interchangeable with the Myriad, the Revelry, or every unsanctioned business in the Empire.

## 4. Xhorhasian and frontier groups

### Kryn Dynasty — Public geopolitical fact; internal truth known later

The Kryn Dynasty is the major power in northern Xhorhas, ruled by the Bright Queen and supported by the Aurora Watch. To Imperial citizens, it is often a military threat described through fear and propaganda. To Dynasty citizens, it is home, religion, hierarchy, protection, and a political order with its own tensions.

### Aurora Watch — Known later in practical detail

The Aurora Watch is the Dynasty's military force. Use its appearance, command structure, and individual conduct only when the party has encountered it or when a source establishes the detail.

### Children of Malice — Author-only until relevant arc

The Children of Malice are a Lolth-aligned drow organization hostile to the Kryn Dynasty and Luxon faith. They are not generic drow antagonists, and their existence should not be used to make every drow suspicious.

## 5. Campaign-secret and later-arc structures

These entries exist only to prevent continuity failures. They must not become early foreshadowing, exposition, or deductions unsupported by the scene.

### Angel of Irons / Tharizdun-related cult structure — Author-only

A hidden religious conspiracy connected to the Chained Oblivion operates through fronts, cells, manipulation, and false belief. Do not reveal its true nature, personnel, or plot before the campaign's evidence reaches it.

### Tombtakers — Author-only until later arc

The Tombtakers are a separate traveling group tied to later northern and Aeorian material. They should not be introduced as a background presence in early Empire scenes.

### Ancient Aeorian interests — Author-only until Eiselcross arc

Multiple states and private actors seek Age of Arcanum knowledge and relics. Specific expeditions, researchers, rivalries, and discoveries should enter only when the campaign reaches Eiselcross and the relevant source material.

## 6. C2E1 local and recurring anchors

### Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities — Public at C2E1

The carnival is a working traveling troupe. Gustav Fletching runs it. Molly performs fortune readings and sword juggling; Yasha works as strongwoman and security.

Treat it as labor: tents, transport, animals, ticket money, weather, route planning, performers, crew relationships, local permits, and reputation. Its later fate must not color C2E1 scenes.

### The Nestled Nook

Yorda runs the inn. Adelaine works as a barmaid. They are venue NPCs first: their knowledge, opinions, and role in a scene come from what they see and what the episode establishes.

### Rinaldo

Rinaldo is a Trostenwald fisherman whose payment to Fjord, Beau, and Jester follows an event before the C2E1 opening. Use only the information that the episode makes public.

## 7. NPC record rule

This file does not replace character dossiers.

For a recurring NPC, create a dedicated dossier only when they have enough scene presence to require stable voice, knowledge control, physical habits, pressure behavior, and a relationship pattern with Mielle.

Until then, record only:

- public role;
- faction or place;
- what the current episode establishes they know;
- durable consequences after approved chapters.

Do not turn a named NPC into an automatic confidant, antagonist, or source of lore.
