# Source adaptation rules

## Transcript role

The Critical Role transcript is the primary source for original canon events, decisions, knowledge flow, tactical intent and consequences.

It is not a prose script, initiative order, dialogue template or battle map.

## Source locator

For Campaign 2 source work, begin with these campaign-wide entry points:

- Official Campaign 2 episode hub: `https://critrole.com/campaign-2-the-mighty-nein/`
- Transcript index on the Critical Role Wiki: `https://criticalrole.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Transcripts`

Locate the relevant transcript by its `Transcript:Campaign 2 Episode [number]` page title. The official hub is the fallback for the episode video when a transcript is unavailable, incomplete, ambiguous, or needs a timing check.

For every extraction or source note, record the exact transcript page or video URL and, for video verification, the relevant timestamp range. Do not cite a recap as a substitute for the episode transcript when the actual source action, order, wording, knowledge state, or tactical intent matters.

## Preserve unless an approved Canon Delta changes it

- outcomes;
- character agency and causal choices;
- information gained, withheld or misunderstood;
- consequences used by later canon;
- major tactical facts when later events depend on them.

## Freely adapt

- initiative and dice mechanics;
- exact action order;
- exact dialogue wording;
- staging, pacing and transitions;
- internal observation;
- minor beats that do not change agency, knowledge or outcome.

## Mielle insertion rule

Mielle may add pressure, options, observations, protection, complications or emotional consequences.

She may not replace a necessary decision or accomplishment of a canon character unless the Canon Delta explicitly permits it.

## Conversation rule

Adapt conversations around the decisions, revelations, refusals, discoveries and relationship changes they must carry. Do not copy lines merely because they appeared in the transcript.

## Combat rule

Adapt combat through pressure waves and changing situation, not turn-by-turn action. Preserve tactical intent and consequences, not dice results or initiative order.
