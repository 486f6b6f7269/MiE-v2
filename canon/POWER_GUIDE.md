# Mielle power guide

## Purpose

This package is the operational source for Mielle’s recovered v2 inventory.

It reconstructs the former `mielle_current_power_guide/` as a complete reference without importing the old project’s current-state labels, recovery percentages, tier ceilings, visibility doctrine, or user-directed access protocol.

The package records what each selected capability does, how it is expressed, where source mechanics stop, and what cannot be invented merely because a franchise name is familiar.

## V2 inventory rule

Every entry in the four modules is part of Mielle’s recovered inventory in v2.

This package does **not** use labels such as `confirmed active`, `active but bounded`, `present / unshown`, `latent`, `seal-gated`, or `peak-only`.

Instead, each entry is handled through four distinctions:

1. **Defined mechanics:** the source description states an effect. It may be used according to that effect.
2. **Source gaps:** the source does not give a range, output, trigger, manifestation, or interaction. Do not invent one.
3. **Physical availability:** an item may exist in Mielle’s inventory but remain inaccessible because it is not among the C2E1 equipment she can physically retrieve.
4. **Ethical and narrative boundaries:** a capability does not create authority to override consent, erase another character’s agency, manufacture evidence, or replace a required canon decision.

## C2E1 equipment access

Nearly all pre-seal equipment remains in Mielle’s pocket dimension and is not physically accessible at story start.

The accessible core equipment is:

- one ordinary wooden bokken bound to her soul;
- Gale-Force Reading Glasses;
- Perfect Wardrobe.

The Bag of Holding is available as dimensional storage, but its accessible contents at C2E1 are limited to those three items. It does not generate supplies, weapons, money, food, maps, medicine, tools, or a safe route out of a scene.

Mielle may use a backpack made through Perfect Wardrobe as social camouflage before retrieving an item. The backpack is not the power’s source or a requirement for retrieval.

## What this package does not import

- the old v1 state labels or progression locks;
- old recovery doctrine or “Tier 2” combat limits;
- equipment not physically accessible at C2E1;
- full franchise kits beyond the mechanics stated in these modules;
- author-side system names in story dialogue or narration;
- an automatic explanation for an undefined manifestation, such as the Stand’s appearance or exact ability.

## Ethics and agency

Cognition, insight, Haki pressure, Eurus, Moriarty, social perception, memory archives and strategic planning never authorize coerced consent, loyalty, romance, confession, obedience, or moral ownership of another person’s choice.

Ares and Steel, Mud, and Faith are inherited memory archives. Mielle does not claim she personally lived every archived war.

## Required full load

Reading this root file alone is not enough when a scene may use a named capability. Read the relevant module or modules:

1. `mielle_current_power_guide/01_current_inventory_01.md`
2. `mielle_current_power_guide/02_current_inventory_02.md`
3. `mielle_current_power_guide/03_fusions_and_upgrades.md`
4. `mielle_current_power_guide/04_consolidated_architecture.md`

For ordinary scenes, load only the entries that can materially affect the scene. For combat, magic, identity threats, complex planning, unusual gear use, or an undefined power expression, load the relevant module plus `canon/HOLMES_ENGINE.md` and `canon/COMBAT_RULES.md` when applicable.

## Scene-use sequence

Before using a named capability:

1. Identify the actual source entry and its stated core effect.
2. State what Mielle is trying to do in the present scene.
3. Check trigger, target, range, duration, cost, prerequisite and source gaps.
4. Apply only the mechanic that is actually described.
5. Keep evidence, physical geometry, consent, source obligations and other characters’ agency intact.
6. After approval, record any new stable interpretation in continuity rather than changing source mechanics silently.

## Language boundary

Mielle understands Common and Zemnian at story start. No cognitive, linguistic or reading capability in this package overrides the established fact that she does not automatically understand Draconic, Elvish, or other Exandrian languages.
