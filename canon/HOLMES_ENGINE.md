# Holmes observation and deduction engine

## Function

Mielle reads the world continuously. The prose decides what part of that reading matters, what stays private, what becomes action and when another person owns the decision.

This file governs observation, inference, testing, fair reveal and the handoff of agency. It is adapted from the former project’s Holmes Engine without importing later-story continuity.

## Evidence boundary

Keep four layers separate:

1. Observation: what Mielle directly perceives.
2. Condition or mechanism: what could produce it.
3. Inference: the narrow conclusion supported by it.
4. Hypothesis: the larger explanation still open to test.

A clue does not prove motive, private thought, moral character or an entire hidden history. Mielle may be unusually fast and accurate, but unsupported claims remain unsupported.

## Confidence

- Certain: directly observed or confirmed.
- High confidence: a decisive anchor or very small dense clue set supports a narrow conclusion.
- Working: useful enough for a low-risk test, warning or preparation.
- Possible: one explanation among several.
- Unknown: identify the gap and stop.

## Scene loop

For a substantial Mielle-lens scene, use:

Frame → Select / weight → Model → Test or withhold → Commit → Reply or revision → Release → Remainder.

- Frame the ordinary condition and let one anomaly disturb it.
- Select only details that matter.
- Build the smallest honest model.
- Test it, wait, ask a discriminating question or withhold for a real reason.
- Commit only to the smallest justified action.
- Let the scene answer or complicate her model.
- Return the decision to the person who owns it.
- End on a concrete remainder whose meaning changed.

## Privacy and consent

People are not evidence objects. For personal observations, distinguish a visible condition from its unknown cause; offer an option rather than a diagnosis; let the person decline, explain, lie or remain private. A correct deduction does not authorize exposure.

## Combat interface

In meaningful combat, Mielle reads body, weapon, spell, terrain and repeated choice. She does not read hearts or hidden motives. Use `canon/COMBAT_RULES.md` for the board, pressure waves and ensemble agency.

## On-page use

Do not name systems in-story. Show the detail she keeps returning to, the pause before a useful question, the route held in her mind, or the movement she makes before danger fully appears.

In close Mielle POV, do not hide a material clue she clearly perceived merely to cheat the reader.

## Revision

Mielle may revise when new evidence arrives or a reasonable premise proves wrong. Her correction should identify what remains true and what changed; do not make her wrong by ignoring visible evidence merely to humble her.
