# Production workflows

## Governing rule

A plan does not become canon. A draft does not update memory. An audit does not rewrite story facts. Only approved chapter text may promote facts into continuity.

## Mandatory cross-chat load

Before every production step:

1. read `START_HERE.md`;
2. read `TASK_LOAD_MATRIX.md`;
3. read `workflow/03_PLANNING_OBLIGATION_LEVELS.md`;
4. read the relevant section of this workflow;
5. create an exact path list from the Matrix and active chapter artifacts.

Do not write `relevant files`, `stable canon`, `core files` or `as needed` in a work artifact. A condition may be marked `not applicable` only with a short reason.

A prior chat's confidence, summary or completed checkbox is not a replacement for this load.

## Obligation-strength rule

Repetition across a plan, mini delta, scene card, pre-check, loadout, guard, draft and audit does not raise authority.

Use `workflow/03_PLANNING_OBLIGATION_LEVELS.md` throughout the workflow:

- Level 1 hard authority, Level 2 required semantic outcomes and Level 3 protective constraints may block when violated;
- Level 4 editorial targets guide craft but require demonstrated local damage before becoming an issue;
- Level 5 execution choices remain free unless they create a continuity, authority, mechanics, knowledge, ownership, relationship, physical-coherence or voice failure.

Every chapter plan and mini delta must classify result-bearing instructions and important craft targets. Scene cards, combat pre-checks, loadouts, guards, drafts, audits and revisions must preserve those levels.

A template field cannot silently strengthen an instruction. Blanket language such as `required`, `closed`, `active`, `removal failure blocks` or `every decision-relevant character needs a consequence` is interpreted through the assigned obligation level and presence class.

A missing obligation map, a loadout that does not verify preservation, or an audit that ranks distance from planning instead of actual damage blocks prose authorization or audit closure.

## Load rules

If a scene contains combat, pursuit, physical threat, power use, power non-use, gear use, injury, no-hit result, evasion, resistance or tactical prediction, read the exact Power Guide modules, Combat Rules and Holmes Engine before prose or revision touches the scene.

If Mielle appears, speaks, decides, reacts, is viewed or is physically affected, read her dossier and current state. Apply current knowledge, gear, personality, voice, drawbacks, constraints/geasa and public presentation.

If a canon cast member appears, speaks, reacts, fights, witnesses, withholds or is used as scene presence, read that character's dossier before writing or revising the line or action. Do not write cast voices from memory or from a prior draft alone.

## Decision-to-authority rule

An exact path list proves only that files were opened. It does not prove that their rules were reconciled.

Before drafting, the Chapter Loadout must contain a completed decision-to-authority check for every Level 1–3 decision, material fact, visible reaction, item state, power use or deliberate non-use that could be governed by more than one source.

Each row must name:

1. the planned fact or decision;
2. its obligation level;
3. the source or planning authority;
4. the governing permanent rule or mechanic;
5. continuity and witness limits;
6. what prose may show;
7. what prose must not imply.

A conflict or missing authority blocks prose. Return it to the owner: source note, Arc Map, Episode Canon Delta, chapter plan, mini delta, permanent canon file or continuity record. Do not solve it by guessing in the draft.

## Character-presence rule

Use four classes:

- required/result-bearing;
- meaningful witness;
- optional texture;
- background.

Physical presence does not require dialogue.

Required/result-bearing characters need owned consequence or restrictive boundary. Meaningful witnesses need knowledge control and may remain silent. Optional texture may be removable when character-specific, authority-safe, non-repetitive and proportionate. Background presence needs no manufactured beat.

Removal and replacement tests become blockers only for required/result-bearing beats or when optional beats accumulate into demonstrated coverage, repeated-function or flattened-dialogue damage.

## Anti-AI application rule

An exact Anti-AI path or checked read box proves only that the filter was opened.

Before prose, the loadout and pre-draft guard must identify chapter-specific failure pressure. Each risk must name:

1. scene trigger;
2. protected fact, voice, uncertainty, awkwardness or causal detail;
3. visible pattern the audit must inspect;
4. obligation level at risk;
5. any justified targeted Anti-AI cluster.

After prose exists, every complete audit must contain the evidence-based Anti-AI receipt from `templates/AUDIT_TEMPLATE.md`. It must:

- return to every pre-draft risk;
- check the seven universal clusters;
- select only justified targeted clusters;
- cite local evidence or an exact inspected pattern;
- group repeated symptoms by root cause;
- record the smallest safe correction or a specific leave-alone reason;
- identify protected material;
- compare versions after substantial revision;
- distinguish lost Level 1–3 facts from neutral Level 4–5 execution changes;
- reread changed paragraphs for new damage.

`Anti-AI: pass`, `filter read`, `checked`, `complete`, `no issues`, or equivalent unsupported wording is invalid.

## 1. Verify source

- Use: when a canon fact, order, consequence, knowledge state or source action is uncertain.
- Read: Base production load; active transcript/VOD; relevant approved chapter; current state; ledger; event index and tiles; exact setting, character, power, Holmes or combat files bearing on the question.
- May change: source note only.
- Output: question, passage, confidence, unresolved point and exact files.
- Must not: write prose, decide a Canon Delta or treat transcript absence as proof of impossibility.

## 2. Extract transcript

- Use: beginning an episode.
- Read: Base production load; active source; current state; recent chain; last approved chapter; starting state when applicable; exact setting files needed to separate source fact from background.
- May change: `work/c2eX/TRANSCRIPT_EXTRACTION.md` only.
- Output: source obligations, owners, information state, causal sequence, tactical intent and later-dependent consequences.
- Must not: write prose or decide Mielle's exact insertion.

## 3. Map arc

- Use: before the first Episode Map of a new arc; reuse and amend only when verified source or approved continuity changes the cross-episode picture.
- Read: Base load; transcript extractions/source notes; continuity; open threads; event index/tiles; approved chapters; relevant dossiers/relationships/settings; mechanics when cross-episode constraints depend on them.
- May change: controlling Arc Map only.
- Output: scope, entry/exit state, causal chain, delayed revelations, ownership, material continuity and episode functions.
- Must not: divide chapters, write scenes/dialogue, decide exact power use or create canon changes.

## 4. Map episode

- Use: after extraction and controlling Arc Map.
- Read: Base load; Arc Map; extraction; continuity; open threads; event index; relevant dossiers, relationships, settings, tiles and ledger.
- May change: Episode Map only.
- Output: chapter divisions, pressure, source beats, agency lanes, information movement and consequences within the Arc Map function.
- Must not: establish a major change, leak later revelation or decide unsupported power answers.

## 5. Episode Canon Delta

- Use: after Episode Map and before chapter plans.
- Read: Base load; Arc Map; extraction; Episode Map; continuity; Mielle dossier/state; relevant cast/relationship/event/knowledge/setting files; mechanics whenever intervention touches them.
- May change: episode Canon Delta only.
- Output: Mielle-caused changes, preserved beats, honest outcome review, protected agency, knowledge effects, mechanics and promotion consequences.
- Must not: preserve source by inertia when established causality changes it or use undefined mechanics as a bridge.

## 6. Plan chapter

- Use: before a chapter.
- Read: Base load including `workflow/03`; Arc Map; Episode Map; Canon Delta; source slice; continuity; recent chain; open threads; event index; last approved chapter; exact relevant character, relationship, setting, event, knowledge, power, Holmes and combat files.
- May change: chapter plan only.
- Output: obligation map, chapter contract, POV, causal chain, decisions, ownership, knowledge/relationship boundaries, end state and carry-forward candidates.
- Must not: write final dialogue, update continuity, force a turn order or classify a preference as hard authority.

## 7. Chapter mini delta

- Use: after every chapter plan and before scene cards.
- Read: Base load; chapter plan; Arc Map; Canon Delta; Episode Map; source slice; continuity; every exact document governing intervention.
- May change: chapter mini delta only.
- Output: localized obligation map, allowed/prohibited actions, protected agency, knowledge flow, mechanics, social boundaries and approval-gated consequences.
- Must not: create a new episode/arc change or strengthen the plan's obligation levels.

## 8. Create scene cards and canon response pass

- Use: when a chapter contains relevant interaction.
- Read: Base load; plan; mini delta; Arc Map where needed; dossiers/states; relationships; ledger; event index/tiles; settings; Holmes where interpretation matters.
- May change: scene cards only.
- Output: obligation preservation, board, required/result-bearing owners, meaningful witnesses, optional texture, background, knowledge limits, independent action, Holmes map and concrete remainder.
- Must not: require every present character to speak, promote optional texture into a result, leak reader-only knowledge or turn dialogue into author-summary collection.

## 9. Combat pre-check

- Use: combat, pursuit, threat, hostile magic, meaningful gear, consequential power, injury, evasion, no-hit or damage.
- Read: Base load; plan; mini delta; scene cards; source slice; dossiers/states; Combat Rules; exact Power Guide modules; Holmes; terrain/settings.
- May change: pre-check/translation card only.
- Output: board, objectives, source obligations, pressure waves, tactical truth, mechanics, ownership and aftermath with preserved obligation levels.
- Must not: create a turn list, let Mielle solve every threat or choose unsupported mechanics.

## 10. Chapter loadout

- Use: immediately before prose.
- Read: Base load; Arc Map; plan; mini delta; scene cards; pre-check; event index; all Matrix paths; complete prose/editing package.
- May change: loadout only.
- Output: exact paths; obligation-strength preservation receipt; planning-control receipt; decision-to-authority check; dossier handoff; execution-system handoff; Anti-AI pressure map; pre-draft authorization.
- Must not: authorize prose while levels conflict, promote Level 4–5 choices, use generic Anti-AI warnings or replace exact reads with summaries.

## 11. Draft

- Use: only after loadout, obligation preservation and decision authority pass.
- Read: every loadout path and pre-draft guard.
- Construction order:
  1. factual/continuity pass — Level 1–3 causality, ownership, material facts, knowledge and end state;
  2. character/relationship pass — required owners, witness boundaries, optional texture and background without coverage;
  3. mechanics/Holmes/combat pass — exact physical, inferential and power execution;
  4. prose/voice pass — expression, rhythm, evidence and dialogue only; no new story facts.
- May change: draft only.
- Output: draft, completed pass state and materially changed-paragraph handoff.
- Must not: update continuity, alter canon without delta, begin before authority reconciliation, treat Level 4–5 suggestions as compulsory prose or declare post-draft receipts complete before audit.

## 12. Audit

- Use: after a complete draft.
- Read: Base load; draft; previous version; loadout; guard; plan; mini delta; scene cards; pre-check; source/Arc/Episode/Delta; relevant canon/continuity/setting/character/relationship/event/knowledge/mechanics/prose files.
- May change: audit only.
- Output: obligation-level compliance first; evidence-based execution receipts; issue list with severity, source and correction; complete Anti-AI receipt and second-order verification.
- Must not: invent facts, promote memory, silently revise story decisions, treat Level 4–5 divergence as a blocker without demonstrated damage, omit risks or close on generic `pass` language.

## 13. Revise

- Use: when audit identifies a correction.
- Read: Base load; current/previous draft; audit; every issue source; loadout; exact affected dossiers/states/mechanics; prose and Anti-AI files.
- May change: draft and audit resolution.
- Output: revised draft, resolved receipts, material comparison and reread.
- Must not: introduce canon changes without planning, lose Level 1–3 specificity, promote a craft preference or continue polishing past the stop condition.

## 14. Approve chapter

- Use: only when the user approves the final draft.
- Read: final draft; closed audit; completed receipts; plan; mini delta; `workflow/03`.
- May change: approved chapter area and project state as authorized.
- Output: approved chapter record.
- Must not: approve while receipts or obligation preservation remain open, or promote continuity before approval.

## 15. Promote memory

- Use: only after approval.
- Read: approved chapter; final audit; mini delta; Episode Canon Delta; continuity; event index; affected states/relationships/tiles/ledger/open threads.
- May change: continuity controllers and project state.
- Output: next-chapter-ready continuity.
- Must not: record unused plans, optional staging, editorial targets or omitted ideas as facts.

## 16. Update permanent rule or decision

- Use: when the user makes a new permanent character, setting, adaptation, combat, power or production decision.
- Read: Base load; authority map; owning file; all references that may become stale.
- May change: owner file and necessary references.
- Output: updated owner and current-work impact.
- Must not: duplicate a rule across several files or retroactively alter approved chapters without explicit revision.

## Transition rule

Do not retroactively invalidate approved chapters solely because they predate a new workflow control.

Apply obligation-level controls to every new chapter, every unapproved chapter whose planning is materially rebuilt, and every approved chapter explicitly reopened for material revision. Existing approved facts remain authority.
