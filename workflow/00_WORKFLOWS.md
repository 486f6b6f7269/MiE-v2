# Production workflows

## Governing rule

A plan does not become canon. A draft does not update memory. An audit does not rewrite story facts. Only approved chapter text may promote facts into continuity.

## 1. Verify source

- Use when: a canon fact, order, consequence, knowledge state or source action is uncertain.
- Read: project core files; relevant transcript; approved chapters; relevant event tiles and knowledge ledger.
- May change: a source note in active work.
- Output: source note within the active episode or chapter work.
- Must not: create a Canon Delta, decide prose, or treat absence in the transcript as proof that something never happened.

## 2. Extract transcript

- Use when: beginning an episode.
- Read: transcript, source adaptation rules, last approved chapter, current story state.
- May change: only `work/c2eX/TRANSCRIPT_EXTRACTION.md`.
- Output: source obligations, agency, information state and table artifacts to discard.
- Must not: write prose or decide Mielle’s exact insertion.

## 3. Map episode

- Use when: after transcript extraction.
- Read: extraction, current state, open threads, last approved chapter and relevant character files.
- May change: only `work/c2eX/EPISODE_MAP.md`.
- Output: chapter divisions, pressure, required agency, information movement and chapter consequences.
- Must not: establish a major canon change.

## 4. Canon Delta

- Use when: after Episode Map and before chapter plans.
- Read: extraction, map, canon, continuity, power guide and relevant character files.
- May change: only `work/c2eX/CANON_DELTA.md`.
- Output: explicit changes caused by Mielle, preserved source beats, knowledge effects and consequences for later promotion.
- Must not: replace a canon character’s required agency without explicit permission.

## 5. Plan chapter

- Use when: before a chapter.
- Read: map, delta, recent story chain, current state, open threads, last approved chapter and relevant event tiles.
- May change: only chapter plan.
- Output: chapter contract, causality, required decisions, information that remains uncertain and end state.
- Must not: write final dialogue, update continuity or force a fixed turn order.

## 6. Create scene cards and canon response pass

- Use when: a chapter includes relevant interaction among Mielle and canon characters.
- Read: chapter plan, character dossiers, current character states, relationship state, knowledge ledger and relevant event tiles.
- May change: only scene cards.
- Output: each person’s knowledge, interpretation, want, natural contact point, independent action and conclusion boundary; Mielle’s protected agency.
- Must not: require every present character to speak or make anyone know reader-only facts.

## 7. Combat pre-check

- Use when: combat, pursuit, physical threat or meaningful power use occurs.
- Read: combat rules, power guide, plan, scene cards, delta, relevant transcript and combatant states.
- May change: only pre-check or combat translation card.
- Output: board, objectives, source obligations, pressure waves, tactical truth, agency lanes, Mielle’s allowed intervention and aftermath.
- Must not: produce a turn list or use Mielle to solve every threat.

## 8. Chapter loadout

- Use when: immediately before prose.
- Read: project state and all completed chapter preparation.
- May change: only chapter loadout.
- Output: exact files and sections required for the draft.
- Must not: summarize canon into new facts or replace reading the files listed.

## 9. Draft

- Use when: a complete chapter loadout exists.
- Read: every file named in the loadout.
- May change: draft only.
- Output: `XX_DRAFT.md`.
- Must not: update continuity, alter canon without delta, give characters unsupported knowledge or copy transcript dialogue as prose.

## 10. Audit

- Use when: after a complete draft.
- Read: draft, loadout, plan, scene cards, source extraction, delta, relevant canon and continuity, Anti-AI filter.
- May change: audit only.
- Output: issue list with severity, evidence and required correction.
- Must not: invent facts, promote memory or silently revise story decisions.

## 11. Revise

- Use when: audit identifies a correction.
- Read: draft, audit and sources cited by each issue.
- May change: revised draft and audit resolution status.
- Output: new draft version and resolved audit.
- Must not: introduce a canon change without returning to the needed planning document.

## 12. Approve chapter

- Use when: the user approves the final draft.
- Read: final draft and closed audit.
- May change: `chapters/approved/` and project state.
- Output: approved chapter record.
- Must not: promote continuity before approval.

## 13. Promote memory

- Use when: only after approval.
- Read: approved chapter, final audit, Canon Delta and current continuity.
- May change: current story state, recent chain, event index, event tiles, knowledge ledger, affected character states, relationship state, open threads and project state.
- Output: continuity ready for the next chapter.
- Must not: record an unused plan, omitted scene idea or author intention as a fact.

## 14. Update permanent rule or decision

- Use when: the user makes a new permanent decision about character, setting, adaptation, combat, power or production.
- Read: authority map and the likely owning file.
- May change: the single owner file and necessary references.
- Output: updated owner and a note of current-work impact when relevant.
- Must not: duplicate a rule in several files or retroactively alter approved chapters without explicit revision.
