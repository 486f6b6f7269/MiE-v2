# Planning obligation levels

## Status

This is a mandatory cross-chat production control.

Read it for every chapter-planning, scene-card, loadout, drafting, audit, revision, approval and continuity task. It applies to new chapters and to materially rebuilt work artifacts from older chapters.

When an older workflow or template uses blanket language such as `every active row must be material`, `removal failure blocks`, `closed decision`, `required reaction`, or `every decision-relevant character needs a consequence`, interpret that language through this file. A template cannot silently strengthen an instruction beyond the authority that created it.

## Purpose

This file prevents a planning preference, diagnostic, illustrative example or one possible staging choice from becoming an immutable canon rule merely because it was copied through several work artifacts.

## Governing principles

### Authority does not grow through repetition

A choice written in a plan, mini delta, scene card, loadout, guard, draft and audit is still only as strong as the source that authorized it.

### Work artifacts may apply authority, not manufacture it

A plan may organize an authorized result. A mini delta may localize it. A scene card may stage it. A loadout may reconcile it. An audit may verify it. None of those steps may raise its obligation strength without a new higher-authority decision.

### Exact wording is rarely authority

Unless the newest explicit user decision, approved prose, stable canon or source requires exact wording, audit the delivered meaning rather than whether the draft copied a planning sentence.

### Actual damage controls severity

An audit issue is ranked by the story, voice, causality, knowledge, mechanics or relationship damage visible in the draft, not by distance from one proposed execution.

## Obligation levels

### Level 1 — Hard authority

A fact, boundary or ownership decision fixed by:

- the newest explicit user decision;
- approved chapter text;
- current continuity;
- stable canon or mechanics;
- verified source outcome;
- an authorized Canon Delta.

Examples:

- Jester owns an offer the user explicitly assigned to her;
- no payment occurs;
- an approved legal restriction remains active;
- a power cannot perform an effect outside its documented mechanics;
- a named person owns a source action or final result.

A Level 1 conflict blocks prose or requires return to the owning authority.

### Level 2 — Required semantic outcome

The meaning, knowledge result, agency result or material consequence must reach the page, but exact wording, sentence count, minor order and non-causal staging remain free.

Examples:

- the group learns Mielle used an abandoned building;
- the temporary nature of an offer is clear before acceptance;
- Mielle retains control over disclosure;
- a witness carries prior combat evidence without needing a prescribed line;
- an object remains with the same owner even if prose uses a different noun.

Audit the delivered meaning, not a copied phrase. A paraphrase passes when authority, knowledge, consequence and ownership remain equivalent.

### Level 3 — Protective constraint

This prevents an unauthorized implication, shortcut, leak or transfer. It often passes through omission.

Examples:

- no friendship, romance, debt or party status is promoted;
- no exact shelter location is revealed;
- no capability name or unsupported local knowledge appears;
- no canon character loses source-owned agency;
- an optional joke does not become evidence of a relationship state.

A Level 3 breach blocks when the invalid implication or result actually enters the draft. The prose is not required to announce the constraint.

### Level 4 — Editorial target

A preferred craft direction that may improve rhythm, voice, pressure, clarity or economy but is not a story fact.

Examples:

- uneven cast participation;
- one strong Nott beat rather than several jokes;
- end on a concrete remainder;
- avoid a question-and-answer chain;
- keep one clumsy event rather than repeating the gag;
- prefer `bokken` in Mielle's narration;
- let silence carry a reaction.

Divergence is not automatically a blocker. Audit the actual local effect. Raise an issue only when the draft produces a demonstrated failure such as coverage compulsion, flattened dialogue, lost pressure, repetition, tonal damage, confusion or identity flattening.

### Level 5 — Free execution

Exact wording, metaphor, sentence rhythm, minor movement, optional reaction, object vocabulary, silence, joke wording and non-outcome staging that do not change authority, knowledge, mechanics, relationship status, ownership or material consequence.

A work artifact may suggest an option, but it must not later audit that suggestion as mandatory merely because it was written down.

## Cross-artifact preservation rule

Every chapter artifact must preserve the obligation strength inherited from the previous authority-bearing step.

| Artifact | Mandatory obligation work |
|---|---|
| Chapter plan | classify each result-bearing instruction and important craft target as Level 1–5 |
| Mini delta | preserve the plan's levels; it may localize but not strengthen them |
| Scene cards | separate required/result-bearing lanes from meaningful witness, optional texture and background presence |
| Combat pre-check | preserve ownership, outcome and mechanics levels; tactical staging remains free where not closed |
| Chapter loadout | complete an obligation-strength preservation receipt before prose |
| Pre-draft guard | identify Level 1–3 blockers separately from Level 4 craft pressure and Level 5 freedom |
| Draft | obey Level 1–3; use Level 4 as craft guidance; choose Level 5 naturally |
| Audit | begin with obligation-level compliance before prose diagnostics |
| Revision | repair actual failure without promoting an editorial preference into a new requirement |
| Approval | confirm that no work artifact silently strengthened a lower-level instruction |
| Promotion | promote only approved prose facts, never planning levels or unused options |

## Loadout obligation-strength receipt

Before prose, the Chapter Loadout must verify:

- every Level 1–3 control names its authority source;
- no Level 4 target was copied into a mandatory outcome, exact line or required reaction;
- Level 5 wording and minor staging remain free;
- character presence is classified as required/result-bearing, meaningful witness, optional texture or background;
- removal and replacement tests are interpreted by obligation level;
- a preferred noun, joke, gesture or sentence pattern has not become a lexical prohibition without authority;
- the pre-draft guard separates blockers from craft risks.

A generic `closed vs free checked` statement is invalid.

## Audit obligation-level receipt

Every complete-draft audit must begin by checking:

1. Level 1 hard facts and ownership;
2. Level 2 semantic and knowledge results;
3. Level 3 protective boundaries;
4. Level 4 targets against actual local evidence;
5. Level 5 execution for coherence only.

An audit may not call a Level 4 or Level 5 divergence a blocker solely because it differs from planning.

When the draft uses an alternative execution, the audit must decide whether it is:

- equivalent and valid;
- a craft gain;
- a craft loss supported by evidence;
- a material authority failure;
- a neutral change.

## Audit severity rule

Use severity from actual damage, not from distance from the plan.

- **Blocker:** violates Level 1, Level 2 or Level 3 in a way that changes authority, knowledge, mechanics, relationship state, legal state, causal result, consent, ownership or approval safety.
- **Major:** produces a sustained structural or voice failure that materially weakens the scene but does not change hard story facts.
- **Important:** local clarity, rhythm, evidence or character-specificity problem worth revising.
- **Optional:** preference, alternative staging or line-level taste with no demonstrated damage.

A Level 4 target can become major only when local evidence shows a real cluster. It cannot be called a blocker merely because the draft chose another valid execution.

## Character participation rule

Presence does not create an obligation to speak.

Use these classes:

- **required/result-bearing:** owns a source action, user-assigned decision, causal result, material pressure change or protected boundary;
- **meaningful witness:** requires knowledge control but may remain silent;
- **optional texture:** may contribute character-specific rhythm, relationship color or pressure without carrying a hard result;
- **background:** physically present without compulsory surface prose.

A required/result-bearing application must retain owned consequence or restrictive boundary.

An optional line may remain even when removable, provided that:

- it is character-specific;
- it does not steal ownership;
- it does not introduce unsupported knowledge;
- it does not repeat a completed function;
- it does not accumulate into cast coverage;
- the plan does not falsely label it required.

Removal and replacement tests are diagnostics. They become blockers only when a supposedly required/result-bearing beat fails them, or when repeated optional beats create a demonstrated structural failure.

## Dossier-workflow interpretation rule

In `workflow/02_CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md`, `templates/SCENE_CARDS_TEMPLATE.md`, `templates/CHAPTER_LOADOUT_TEMPLATE.md`, `templates/AUDIT_TEMPLATE.md` and `templates/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION_TEMPLATE.md`:

- `decision-relevant` means required or result-bearing, not merely speaking;
- optional texture does not need a material consequence to exist;
- a removable optional line is not automatically superficial;
- practical competence must be considered where a required result depends on it, not displayed in every possible scene;
- silence and withdrawal may satisfy character logic;
- cumulative cast coverage is audited at exchange and chapter scale;
- any conflicting blanket blocker statement is narrowed by this interpretation.

## Mielle invariant interpretation rule

An invariant should be activated only when planning expects it to change a result, choice, pressure, knowledge state, object state or restrictive boundary.

Do not activate an invariant merely because a natural minor texture appears. Incidental voice or physical texture may remain Level 4–5 without requiring a material invariant receipt.

Once an invariant is marked active and result-bearing, its planned consequence or blocked result must be present unless the plan is revised.

## Wording and vocabulary rule

A preferred word is not a lexical prohibition unless stable canon or an explicit user decision says it is.

Audit whether wording changes identity, knowledge, object continuity, mechanics or meaning. Do not block a natural synonym that preserves the authorized result.

## Version-comparison rule

When comparing drafts, distinguish:

- lost Level 1 fact or ownership;
- lost Level 2 semantic result;
- changed Level 3 boundary;
- changed but equivalent wording;
- editorial gain or loss;
- neutral execution change.

Do not label every difference specificity regression.

## Cross-chat reproducibility gate

A new chat or agent may not authorize prose or close an audit until it has:

- read `START_HERE.md`;
- read `TASK_LOAD_MATRIX.md`;
- read this file;
- read the relevant section of `workflow/00_WORKFLOWS.md`;
- identified the exact chapter authority sources;
- checked existing planning for accidental obligation strengthening;
- completed or verified the required obligation map and loadout receipt.

This gate applies even when another chat already worked on the chapter. Prior chat confidence is not a receipt.

## Existing and approved material

Do not retroactively invalidate approved chapters solely because they predate this control.

When an unapproved chapter's planning is materially rebuilt, apply this file to the rebuilt artifacts.

When an approved chapter is explicitly reopened for material revision, preserve its approved facts as authority and apply this file to new choices and changed passages.

## Stop condition

Planning is ready when:

- Level 1–3 controls are clear, sourced and non-conflicting;
- Level 4 targets are not disguised as immutable results;
- Level 5 choices remain genuinely free;
- character participation is pressure-selected rather than form-selected;
- loadout and pre-draft guard preserve the levels;
- the future audit can distinguish factual failure from editorial preference.

An audit is ready to close when:

- obligation levels were checked before prose diagnostics;
- no lower-level choice was silently promoted;
- actual failures are supported by local evidence;
- optional texture was assessed cumulatively rather than rejected by automatic removal;
- version comparison distinguishes lost facts from neutral execution changes.
