# Character dossier application workflow

## Purpose

This workflow extension prevents `canon/characters/` dossiers from being reduced to voice labels, one plausible line, a reaction to Mielle or a decorative habit.

Read it with:

- `workflow/00_WORKFLOWS.md`;
- `workflow/01_EXECUTION_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md`;
- `canon/characters/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION.md`;
- `templates/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION_TEMPLATE.md`;
- `canon/prose/CHAPTER_PROSE_AND_CHARACTER_VOICE_PASS.md`.

## Governing rule

The Character Voice Pass proves how a character expresses pressure. The Character Dossier Application receipt proves why the character acts, what they can do, what they cannot know, what existing tie they protect, and what materially changes because they are present.

Neither receipt replaces the other.

## Before prose

### 1. Classify presence

For every physically present character, choose one:

- decision-relevant;
- source-action owner;
- result owner;
- meaningful witness;
- intentionally background.

Do not create an action or line merely because a name appears in the cast list.

### 2. Synthesize stable and current state

For each decision-relevant character, read:

- the stable dossier;
- relevant event tiles;
- knowledge-ledger entries;
- relationship state;
- current character-state file when one exists;
- latest approved chapter or recent story chain when needed.

Record:

- current campaign phase;
- approved-event residue;
- immediate want;
- knowledge and spoiler limit;
- existing ties and task before Mielle;
- pressure behavior;
- practical competence;
- prohibited reduction.

### 3. Assign a consequence

Each decision-relevant dossier must have either:

- a productive consequence that changes action, pressure, timing, information, object state, available choice, tactical line or outcome; or
- a restrictive consequence that blocks a tempting invalid result and leaves a concrete replacement or omission.

If no consequence exists, classify the character as background or return to planning.

### 4. Preserve existing ties

Before assigning Mielle contact, identify the character's source-owned priorities and relationships. Mielle may redirect or complicate them; she may not erase them.

## During drafting

For each decision-relevant character:

- write from immediate attention and want, not from dossier summary;
- let pressure alter timing, action and error, not only word choice;
- use practical competence where the scene naturally permits it;
- preserve knowledge and spoiler boundaries;
- allow silence, withdrawal or refusal;
- avoid giving every character a beat for coverage;
- keep ownership of source actions and results visible.

Record representative passages for the later dossier receipt.

## Audit

Create or complete a chapter-specific receipt from `templates/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION_TEMPLATE.md`.

The audit must verify:

1. stable dossier phase;
2. current approved-event state;
3. existing ties and task;
4. immediate attention and want;
5. pressure behavior;
6. practical competence or restrictive boundary;
7. owned expression;
8. material consequence;
9. knowledge and spoiler limit;
10. prohibited reduction;
11. removal test;
12. replacement test;
13. revision comparison and second-order reread where applicable.

Local draft evidence is required. Planning labels alone do not pass.

## Relationship to Character Voice Pass

The same passage may support both receipts, but the questions differ.

- Voice Pass: does the line, action or silence sound and move like this character under this pressure?
- Dossier Application: does the character's complete logic materially shape the scene, preserve current phase and ties, and resist replacement by another character?

A voice-accurate but causally unnecessary line can pass Voice Pass and fail Dossier Application.

## Relationship to scene cards

Scene cards must list only decision-relevant characters in the detailed dossier application table. Background presence belongs in the board and witness state without compulsory action.

The fixed main-cast roll call is prohibited. Add rows only for characters whose dossier logic must change or restrict the scene.

## Relationship to continuity

Stable dossiers point to `continuity/EVENT_INDEX.md`; they do not duplicate an expanding event list.

When approved events create a durable character-specific change that cannot be reliably reconstructed from event tiles, ledger and relationship state, create or rebuild a current character-state file through the existing workflow.

## Revision

After substantial revision:

- compare representative before/after passages;
- check whether practical competence was removed or transferred;
- check whether a unique roughness, mistake or pressure response was polished away;
- check whether later knowledge or maturity leaked in;
- check whether existing ties weakened only to center Mielle;
- check whether new cast-coverage beats appeared;
- rerun removal and replacement tests.

## Blockers

Audit closure and chapter approval are blocked when:

- the receipt is absent for a chapter with decision-relevant canon characters;
- a character is supported only by a file path, trait, accent or voice label;
- a decision-relevant character has no productive or restrictive consequence;
- current approved-event residue is omitted or contradicted;
- removal leaves the scene unchanged without intentional background classification;
- replacement by another character is possible without justification;
- practical competence is ignored despite a natural opening;
- later-canon knowledge, maturity or relationship state leaks backward;
- the Mielle contact pattern replaces existing ties and task;
- all present characters receive compulsory reactions;
- revision lacks comparison or second-order reread.

An explicit user waiver applies only when it names the Character Dossier Application receipt and the chapter. General approval does not silently waive it.

## Approved chapters

Do not retroactively invalidate approved chapters solely because they predate this workflow.

If an approved chapter is materially revised, apply this receipt to every character affected by the revision. Existing approved facts remain authority unless the user explicitly reopens them.
