# Character dossier application workflow

## Purpose

This workflow extension prevents `canon/characters/` dossiers from being reduced to voice labels, one plausible line, a reaction to Mielle or a decorative habit.

Read it with:

- `workflow/00_WORKFLOWS.md`;
- `workflow/01_EXECUTION_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md`;
- `workflow/03_PLANNING_OBLIGATION_LEVELS.md`;
- `canon/characters/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION.md`;
- `templates/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION_TEMPLATE.md`;
- `canon/prose/CHAPTER_PROSE_AND_CHARACTER_VOICE_PASS.md`.

## Governing rule

The Character Voice Pass proves how a character expresses pressure. The Character Dossier Application receipt proves why a required or result-bearing character acts, what they can do, what they cannot know, what existing tie they protect, and what changes or is prevented because they are present.

Neither receipt replaces the other.

`workflow/03_PLANNING_OBLIGATION_LEVELS.md` controls how removal, replacement, consequence and blocker language is interpreted. Optional texture is not promoted into a required dossier application merely because the character speaks.

## Presence classes

Before assigning detailed dossier work, classify every physically present character as one of:

- **required/result-bearing:** owns a source action, user-assigned decision, material consequence, protected boundary or causal pressure change;
- **meaningful witness:** later knowledge matters, but no line or action is required;
- **optional texture:** may contribute one character-specific beat without owning a hard result;
- **background:** physically present without compulsory surface prose.

Do not create an action or line merely because a name appears in the cast list.

A character is not `required/result-bearing` merely because the current draft gave them dialogue. Classify from authority and causal need before using the draft as evidence.

## Before prose

### 1. Load obligation strength

For each planned character beat, record:

- authority source;
- obligation level from `workflow/03_PLANNING_OBLIGATION_LEVELS.md`;
- required meaning or protected boundary;
- free execution;
- audit consequence.

Do not let a dossier suggestion, voice example or optional contact pattern become Level 1–3 without authority.

### 2. Synthesize stable and current state

For each required/result-bearing character, read:

- the stable dossier;
- relevant event tiles;
- knowledge-ledger entries;
- relationship state;
- current character-state file when one exists;
- latest approved chapter or recent story chain when needed.

Record:

- current campaign phase;
- approved-event residue;
- immediate want;
- knowledge and spoiler limit;
- existing ties and task before Mielle;
- pressure behavior;
- practical competence relevant to the required result;
- prohibited reduction.

Meaningful witnesses need knowledge and conclusion boundaries. Optional texture needs a natural trigger, character ownership and a cumulative-coverage check. Background presence needs no manufactured beat.

### 3. Assign consequences only where authority requires them

Each required/result-bearing dossier application must have either:

- a productive consequence that changes action, pressure, timing, information, object state, available choice, tactical line or outcome; or
- a restrictive consequence that blocks a tempting invalid result and leaves a concrete replacement or omission.

If no required consequence exists, reclassify the character as meaningful witness, optional texture or background. Do not invent a consequence to keep them in the detailed table.

An optional texture beat may be removable and still valid when it is character-specific, knowledge-safe, ownership-safe, non-repetitive and does not accumulate into coverage.

### 4. Preserve existing ties

Before assigning Mielle contact, identify the character's source-owned priorities and relationships. Mielle may redirect or complicate them; she may not erase them.

## During drafting

For each required/result-bearing character:

- write from immediate attention and want, not from dossier summary;
- let pressure alter timing, action and error, not only word choice;
- use practical competence when the required result naturally depends on it;
- preserve knowledge and spoiler boundaries;
- allow silence, withdrawal or refusal;
- keep ownership of source actions and results visible.

For meaningful witnesses:

- preserve line of sight, memory and conclusion limits;
- do not force a reaction merely to prove they were present.

For optional texture:

- use a natural trigger;
- keep the beat character-specific;
- stop when its local function lands;
- check the whole exchange for cast-roll-call accumulation.

Record representative passages for the later dossier receipt.

## Audit

Create or complete a chapter-specific receipt from `templates/CHARACTER_DOSSIER_APPLICATION_TEMPLATE.md`.

The audit must begin by preserving the planning obligation level of each character application.

For every required/result-bearing application, verify:

1. stable dossier phase;
2. current approved-event state;
3. existing ties and task;
4. immediate attention and want;
5. pressure behavior;
6. practical competence or restrictive boundary;
7. owned expression;
8. productive consequence or protected boundary;
9. knowledge and spoiler limit;
10. prohibited reduction;
11. removal test;
12. replacement test;
13. revision comparison and second-order reread where applicable.

For meaningful witnesses, verify knowledge and conclusion boundaries.

For optional texture, verify:

- natural trigger;
- character-specific ownership;
- no unsupported knowledge;
- no ownership theft;
- no repeated function;
- no cumulative cast coverage.

Local draft evidence is required. Planning labels alone do not pass.

## Removal and replacement interpretation

### Required/result-bearing beat

Removal or interchangeability is a potential blocker because the beat claims causal necessity or protected ownership.

- If removal leaves the required action, pressure, choice, knowledge, object state, timing or result unchanged, return to planning or reclassify it.
- If another character can perform the same action and consequence unchanged, justify unique ownership or revise.

### Meaningful witness

A witness may remain silent. Removal is tested against later knowledge and continuity, not against immediate dialogue volume.

### Optional texture

A removable optional beat is not automatically superficial. Treat interchangeability as a voice warning, not a story blocker. Raise an issue only when the beat is generic, knowledge-unsafe, ownership-stealing, repetitive or part of a demonstrated coverage cluster.

### Background

No removal justification is required unless later continuity depends on the person's presence.

## Relationship to Character Voice Pass

The same passage may support both receipts, but the questions differ.

- Voice Pass: does the line, action or silence sound and move like this character under this pressure?
- Dossier Application: does a required/result-bearing character's complete logic shape the result or protected boundary, and does optional texture remain character-specific without becoming coverage?

A voice-accurate but causally unnecessary line may still be valid optional texture. It fails only when it claims required status, steals ownership, leaks knowledge, flattens voice or accumulates into a structural problem.

## Relationship to scene cards

Scene cards must separate:

- required/result-bearing rows;
- meaningful witness rows;
- optional texture lanes;
- background presence.

Only required/result-bearing characters need the full consequence chain.

The fixed main-cast roll call is prohibited. Add detailed rows only for characters whose dossier logic must change or restrict the scene.

## Relationship to continuity

Stable dossiers point to `continuity/EVENT_INDEX.md`; they do not duplicate an expanding event list.

When approved events create a durable character-specific change that cannot be reliably reconstructed from event tiles, ledger and relationship state, create or rebuild a current character-state file through the existing workflow.

## Revision

After substantial revision:

- compare representative before/after passages;
- check whether a required practical competence was removed or transferred;
- check whether unique roughness, mistake or pressure response was polished away;
- check whether later knowledge or maturity leaked in;
- check whether existing ties weakened only to center Mielle;
- check whether new cast-coverage beats appeared;
- rerun removal and replacement tests for required/result-bearing beats;
- recheck optional texture cumulatively rather than rejecting each removable line in isolation;
- confirm that no Level 4 or Level 5 choice was promoted during revision.

## Blockers

Audit closure and chapter approval are blocked when:

- the receipt is absent for a chapter with required/result-bearing canon-character applications;
- a required application is supported only by a file path, trait, accent or voice label;
- a required/result-bearing character has no productive consequence or restrictive boundary;
- current approved-event residue is omitted or contradicted;
- a required/result-bearing beat fails removal without reclassification;
- required ownership is replaceable without justification;
- practical competence required for the result is ignored;
- later-canon knowledge, maturity or relationship state leaks backward;
- the Mielle contact pattern replaces existing ties and task;
- all present characters receive compulsory reactions;
- optional texture accumulates into a demonstrated cast-roll-call, repeated-function or flattened-dialogue cluster;
- revision lacks required comparison or second-order reread;
- the audit silently promotes a Level 4 or Level 5 choice into a blocker.

The following are not blockers by themselves:

- a meaningful witness remaining silent;
- an intentionally background character lacking a beat;
- a removable optional texture line;
- unused practical competence when the scene offers no natural or result-bearing lane;
- a different but equivalent wording or minor staging choice.

An explicit user waiver applies only when it names the Character Dossier Application receipt and the chapter. General approval does not silently waive it.

## Approved chapters

Do not retroactively invalidate approved chapters solely because they predate this workflow.

If an approved chapter is materially revised, apply this receipt to every required/result-bearing character affected by the revision. Existing approved facts remain authority unless the user explicitly reopens them.
