## Episode baseline at opening

Trostenwald is a working Imperial road town. The episode begins in the Nestled Nook on the morning after separate arrivals and prior off-screen events. Caleb and Nott are keeping themselves inconspicuous after staying upstairs. Fjord, Jester and Beau have already helped Rinaldo's daughter and are waiting for his promised payment. Molly and Yasha are in town with the Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities.

Mielle is not an original-source participant. Her approved pre-C2E1 baseline places her nearby, hungry, without local monetary knowledge, and entering the inn only to learn whether her few coins can buy food. That is adaptation input, not a source event.

## Source obligations

- Outcomes that must remain true:
  - The future Mighty Nein meet at the Nestled Nook as separate, initially wary people.
  - Rinaldo pays Fjord, Jester and Beau for their earlier rescue.
  - Molly and Yasha advertise the carnival; Molly's invitation gives the group a reason to attend.
  - The old man and woman transform during the performance.
  - The party stops both threats.
  - The Crownsguard investigation keeps the carnival and group in Trostenwald.

- Decisions that must remain character-owned:
  - Caleb and Nott decide how much of themselves to reveal and how close to stand to the others.
  - Jester chooses to initiate social contact and to include people.
  - Fjord, Beau and Nott decide their own degree of caution, challenge and participation.
  - Molly chooses to invite and perform; Yasha chooses her practical role at the carnival.
  - Each canon combatant retains meaningful combat choices in the carnival fight.
  - The Crownsguard retain the official decision to hold people for investigation.

- Information gained:
  - The tavern group learns names, visible roles, the carnival's existence, and that the others are travelling toward or through the same region.
  - The group sees that Nott is a goblin and that Caleb is a wizard; neither disclosure creates full biography or automatic trust.
  - The crowd and group directly witness the transformations and the fight's public aftermath.

- Information withheld or misunderstood:
  - The private histories of Caleb, Nott, Fjord, Beau, Molly and Yasha remain private.
  - The cause, identity and wider meaning of the transformations are not solved in C2E1.
  - A source character's visible magical practice does not reveal their deeper power source or loyalties.

- Consequences used later:
  - The group remains in Trostenwald under scrutiny.
  - The carnival's relation to the deaths and the town's authorities continues to matter.
  - The first mutual social tolerance between the future party members is established without becoming instant loyalty.

- Tactical facts with later dependency:
  - The threat begins inside a public carnival performance with civilians nearby.
  - The two hostile transformations create overlapping danger rather than a private duel.
  - The source fight is an ensemble response whose public visibility leads directly to the guard intervention.

## Locations, NPCs and material facts

- The Nestled Nook is an inn in Trostenwald. It provides food and lodging; exact furniture, food, prices, exits and crowd arrangement remain scene-level decisions.
- Rinaldo is the fisherman who pays Fjord, Jester and Beau. His payment belongs to their prior rescue and cannot be reassigned to Mielle.
- Molly and Yasha belong to the Fletching and Moondrop Traveling Carnival of Curiosities. The carnival is a working troupe, not a decorative background.
- Trostenwald is Imperial territory with Crownsguard authority. Public disorder, death and strange magic have institutional consequences.

## Combat and threat facts

- No tavern combat is required by the source.
- The carnival fight begins when two apparent civilians transform in front of an audience.
- The encounter needs civilian exposure, confusion, multiple threats and independent canon-character action.
- The source result is not simply that monsters die. The public event leaves evidence, fear, witnesses and an official investigation.

## Table artifacts to discard

- Initiative or turn-order artifacts: exact initiative, interrupted turns, movement-square sequences and action economy.
- Dice-result artifacts: attack rolls, damage totals, saving throws, failed skill checks and rules arguments.
- Improvised wording that does not carry a decision: line-by-line banter, repeated jokes, exploratory table chatter and recaps that do not alter knowledge, agency or outcome.
- Other: precise spell-slot accounting and exact monster-stat logic unless a later consequence depends on a stated fact.

## Adaptation cautions

- Mielle's tavern contact must not absorb the original first-contact lane among the future Mighty Nein.
- The transcript does not authorize anyone to infer Mielle's origin, sealed memory, power ceiling, pocket storage or emotional needs.
- Mielle may not be placed at the carnival fight without a later Delta review of every outcome she could alter. Until then, extraction records only that the source fight is public, ensemble-dependent and consequential.
- Do not treat the group's willingness to share a carnival visit as proof of permanent trust or a settled party.
- Do not use later canon to explain the monsters, predict the investigation's result, or make any C2E1 character emotionally wiser than the source supports.
