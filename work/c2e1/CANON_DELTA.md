## Knowledge effects

### Information created

- Jester may know Mielle's name if given, hunger, unfamiliarity with local coins, a visible wooden sword if shown, a dry or unusually literal manner, and whether Mielle accepted the ticket.
- Nearby people may know only what they directly observe or hear. A person can know Jester paid one admission without inferring that Mielle belongs to Jester or the group.
- Molly and Yasha may know Mielle is a paying guest whose entry was covered by Jester. They do not know why Mielle lacks money beyond what Mielle actually says.

### Information delayed, withheld or still uncertain

- Mielle's origin, sealed memory, power ceiling, storage, shelter location, reason for hunger and actual combat capacity.
- The value of Mielle's coins unless a visible exchange establishes it.
- The cause of the carnival transformations and each canon character's private history.
- Mielle's exact response during the fight. This belongs to the Chapter 03 combat-specific amendment.

### Knowledge that must not leak from later canon

- Later developments and private histories of Caleb, Nott, Fjord, Beau, Molly and Yasha.
- The cause and later meaning of the transformed creatures.
- Later relationship, travel, faction and plot outcomes.

## Mechanics and power constraints

- Relevant capability or equipment: Mielle's ordinary wooden sword, Perfect Wardrobe backpack camouflage, Gale-Force Reading Glasses and Bag of Holding. For Chapters 01 and 02, none of these supplies food, money, ticket payment, special admission or a social shortcut.
- Stated mechanic: accessible storage cannot generate supplies, weapons, money, food, maps, medicine, tools or a safe route. Mielle's paid entry comes from Jester's voluntary choice.
- Source gap or boundary: no named power or cognition effect solves currency, reads thoughts, produces food, creates trust or bypasses carnival procedure. Holmes reasoning cannot turn hunger, clothing, silence, posture or a brief answer into hidden history or motive.
- Visible consequence: food and the ticket come from Jester's voluntary attention. Mielle's acceptance is a choice, not a debt or a proof of party membership.
- Chapter 03 boundary: no mechanic, defensive claim, gear retrieval or combat result is authorized until the required combat-specific Delta amendment and pre-check read the relevant modules.

## Relationship and setting consequences

- A meal, an enjoyable exchange and one paid ticket are not a relationship state, debt, promise, group membership, trust or romance.
- The chapter may establish a first workable rhythm between Jester and Mielle: Jester is curious without pity; Mielle answers a little more than necessity demands; both want to know more. This is a beginning, not an established friendship.
- Molly's invitation is an offer, not recruitment. Jester's ticket is specific to the evening's entry, not ongoing financial support.
- Mielle does not obtain a complete education in prices, law, routes or custom from breakfast or the carnival.

## Later-canon dependency check

- The source group still reaches the carnival and becomes involved in the public incident.
- Mielle reaches the carnival too. She will be a direct witness and cannot later be written as ignorant of the transformations or public aftermath.
- The later combat and investigation need a Chapter 03 review before prose. That review must decide whether Mielle is recorded by the guard as a witness, a combatant, or both.
- The first conversation and paid ticket create no forced party membership, romance, travel agreement, faction attention or ownership.
