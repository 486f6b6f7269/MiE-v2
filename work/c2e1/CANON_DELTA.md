## Knowledge effects

### Information created

- Jester may know that Mielle was hungry, carried a few coins without understanding their value, accepted or hesitated over food, and chose to leave.
- Any other tavern witness knows only what their physical position and the actual chapter text supports. A glance at a red cloak or wooden sword does not create a biography, threat assessment or durable suspicion.
- Molly may know that Mielle is an unusual traveler he invited to the carnival; Mielle may know that Molly is connected to a carnival.

### Information delayed, withheld or still uncertain

- Mielle's origin, sealed memory, power ceiling, dimensional storage, shelter location, reason for hunger and actual combat capacity.
- Whether any character saw the bokken clearly enough to remember it. This is a scene-card question; no one may infer special properties from an ordinary wooden weapon.
- Whether Mielle's coins are enough for a meal, unless a visible exchange establishes a price.
- The cause of the carnival transformations and every later secret attached to the canon characters.

### Knowledge that must not leak from later canon

- The private histories and future developments of Caleb, Nott, Fjord, Beau, Molly and Yasha.
- The cause and wider arc of the transformed creatures.
- Any later relationship, party loyalty, institutional knowledge, travel route or plot resolution.

## Mechanics and power constraints

- Relevant capability or equipment: ordinary soulbound bokken; Perfect Wardrobe backpack camouflage; Gale-Force Reading Glasses exist but are not used; Bag of Holding has no relevant accessible contents beyond the listed C2E1 items.
- Stated mechanic: the accessible items do not generate food, money, maps, medicine, tools or a safe route. A backpack can conceal retrieval but no retrieval is needed in this episode plan.
- Source gap or boundary: no named capability, cognition effect or power interaction may be invoked merely to solve unfamiliar currency, read private thoughts, produce food or bridge a social gap. Holmes reasoning may not turn clothing, hunger or silence into facts about anyone's history or motives.
- Visible consequence: Mielle resolves the immediate food problem through Jester's voluntary choice, not through a hidden resource or a power display.

## Relationship and setting consequences

- A single offered meal is not a relationship state, obligation, debt, promise, adoption into the group, or evidence of lasting trust.
- The second invitation is not a party recruitment. Molly offers an experience; Mielle remains free to decline.
- Imperial-town setting limits matter: Mielle does not understand prices, etiquette, local authority or the full risk of public oddity. The scene may show a small fact, but it must not turn one breakfast into a local education.

## Later-canon dependency check

- The source group still reaches the carnival and becomes involved in the public incident.
- Mielle has no witnessed involvement in the fight, deaths or guard response. Later canon can therefore retain the original witness and investigation structure.
- The added first and second contacts create no forced change to travel, custody, faction attention or romance.
