# Chapter 03 combat scope amendment

## Status

This records the user's correction and subsequent combat decisions after the first Chapter 03 planning pass.

It supersedes any conflicting Chapter 03 work language that:

- treats Mielle's opening containment as the whole fight;
- restricts her to a repeated dodge-and-stall pattern;
- forbids Haki in the chapter;
- limits Armament to minor staggering only;
- leaves Mielle's injury state undecided;
- lets the writer invent later exchanges, exact injuries, kills, casualty count or hierarchy in prose.

The original plan, mini delta, scene cards and first combat pre-check remain usable for source order, crowd geometry, witness limits, source-character agency and legal aftermath. Their combat details are overridden wherever this amendment, `03_NO_HIT_RESOLUTION.md`, `03_COMBAT_PRECHECK_v2.md`, or the revised planning files differ.

## Fixed opening

- The old man transforms during Toya's performance.
- Jester acts first with the source opening magic.
- The first creature attacks the young woman. Mielle is surprised because the transformation and attack are unknown and immediate; the crowd and inward position prevent a clean route before the second strike.
- The woman dies and later becomes the second creature.
- After seeing the death, Mielle walks calmly toward the danger with no bokken in hand. The calm approach is chosen control, not indifference.
- The bokken appears directly in her hand during the approach. It is the same physical bokken that Yasha had held.
- The first creature treats Mielle as a likely next victim because she looks small, harmless and armed only with a wooden sword. Mielle avoids the committed attack by a very small margin and strikes its knee to disrupt its line.
- The second creature attacks. Mielle avoids that initial attack with martial footwork aided by Observation Haki's short warning of intent, vector and timing.

## Fixed middle and finish

- The first knee strike is a test. Mielle feels that the creature is materially harder than a normal human body.
- From the first counter after that realization onward, every counterattack Mielle makes is reinforced by Armament Haki.
- Armament is used for strike reinforcement only. It is not advanced emission, internal destruction, automatic invulnerability, a barrier, a cure, a mind effect or an answer to the transformation mechanism.
- Mielle does not aim at joints as a disabling shortcut. Her Haki counters use whatever limb or opening an actual exchange gives her.
- Across the cooperative fight, Mielle breaks one arm and one leg of each creature with Armament-reinforced bokken counters. These are physical limb breaks, not a hidden weak-point technique, internal destruction or an unexplained special effect.
- At least one major limb break must occur during the cooperative phase after source characters have begun acting. The fight must not place three or four of the breaks before the group meaningfully enters.
- The broken limbs materially reduce each creature's reach, balance or ability to drive through the crowd, but do not end the fight. The creatures remain mobile and dangerous enough to attack, adapt and require the source group to finish them.
- Mielle receives no hit. No bite, claw, grab, body-check, collision or indirect strike lands on her.
- Mielle keeps both creatures occupied through a moving exchange of evasions and Haki-reinforced replies. She is not merely waiting passively, and she is not required to carry the fight alone for an extended source-table interval.
- The source group recovers weapons quickly after seeing Mielle enter the danger. Once they reach the active line, Mielle and the others fight as an improvised team against the two creatures.
- The exact cooperative exchanges remain flexible. They must arise from each person’s chosen position and ability, not from Mielle assigning roles or narrating their motives.
- Jester's opening, Yasha's weapon distribution, meaningful contributions from Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly, Yasha's first-creature kill, Beau's second-creature finish, and the Crownsguard aftermath remain required.
- Mielle does not take either finishing kill. Her Haki counters create real damage and real openings, but cannot silently replace the decisive source actions.

## Exact governing mechanics

- `canon/mielle_current_power_guide/02_current_inventory_02.md`, `## 11. Soulbond`.
- `canon/mielle_current_power_guide/02_current_inventory_02.md`, `## 12. Haki`.
- Soulbond permits recall of the same nonliving bokken; it does not duplicate weapons, bind others' equipment or explain itself to witnesses.
- Observation provides a short combat-prediction warning of immediate hostile intent, line and timing. It is not unlimited future sight, mind-reading, a transformation explanation or a replacement for physical space.
- Armament provides black coating and reinforcement that improves strength and durability. The source supplies no numerical output, duration, coverage, advanced emission or internal-destruction technique.
- In-story prose must not name Haki, Soulbond or their source systems. Visible results may be shown only as concrete physical facts.

## Still flexible

- Exact order of the four limb breaks, except that at least one major break must occur after allies are acting.
- Exact cooperative opening that lets Mielle break the cooperative-phase limb.
- Exact visual extent of Armament coating, constrained by the source module and the need to avoid a system-name explanation.
- Mielle's immediate emotional response after the first surprise, beyond the fact that the death is not trivialized.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- Mielle cannot erase the first death, second transformation, public crowd emergency, source-character agency, Yasha's first-creature kill, Beau's second-creature finish or Crownsguard jurisdiction without a new explicit episode-level decision.
- She cannot gain another capability from implication. Any further power beyond Soulbond recall, Observation Haki and Armament Haki needs an explicit user decision and an exact mechanics check.
- The fight cannot make the source group spectators or turn their entries into a rescue script imposed by Mielle.
- Public recall and visible unusual combat remain witness facts, but do not reveal Mielle's origin, full power ceiling, inventory or system explanation.
- Casualty count is closed by the active scene cards and combat pre-check: old man/first creature and young woman/second creature are the only named dead by chapter end. No extra body may appear in prose without returning to planning.
