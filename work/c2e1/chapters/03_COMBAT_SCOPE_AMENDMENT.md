# Chapter 03 combat scope amendment

## Status

This records the user's correction after the first Chapter 03 planning pass.

It supersedes any conflicting detailed choreography or limit in the current Chapter 03 work artifacts, including language that treats Mielle's opening containment as the whole fight, restricts her to a repeated dodge-and-stall pattern, or chooses later exchanges without a user decision.

The current Chapter 03 plan, mini delta, scene cards and combat pre-check are therefore **provisional**. They remain useful for source order, crowd geometry, witness limits, source-character agency and legal aftermath. They are not yet authorization to draft the middle or end of Mielle's fight.

## What is fixed

- The old man transforms during Toya's performance.
- Jester acts first with the source opening magic.
- The first creature attacks the young woman. Mielle is surprised because the transformation and attack are unknown and immediate; the crowd and inward position prevent a clean route before the second strike.
- The woman dies and later becomes the second creature.
- After seeing the death, Mielle walks calmly toward the danger with no bokken in hand. The calm approach is chosen control, not indifference.
- The bokken appears directly in her hand during the approach. It is the same physical bokken that Yasha had held.
- The first creature treats Mielle as a likely next victim. Mielle avoids the committed attack by a very small margin and strikes its knee to disrupt its line.
- The second creature attacks. Mielle's footwork lets her avoid that initial attack with ease.
- Mielle then holds both creatures' attention long enough for the other characters to reach the fight and make their own choices.
- Jester's opening, Yasha's weapon distribution and first-creature kill, Beau's second-creature finish, meaningful contributions from Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly, and the Crownsguard aftermath remain source requirements.

## What is deliberately unresolved

- The full sequence after the second initial dodge.
- Whether Mielle's subsequent fighting is mostly evasive, includes stronger counters, changes tempo, creates openings, suffers a hit, or uses additional defined capabilities.
- Any additional item, power or technique beyond the visible bokken recall. Nothing is authorized or prohibited merely because an earlier planning document guessed a limit; any added capability needs an explicit user decision and an exact mechanics check.
- The specific exchanges by which Mielle holds two creatures, and the exact moment each ally joins.
- How long Mielle carries the two-creature pressure before the source group changes the board.
- Mielle's emotional response after the immediate surprise, beyond the fact that the death is not treated as trivial.

## Boundaries that remain non-negotiable

- Mielle cannot erase the first death, second transformation, public crowd emergency, source-character agency, Yasha's first-creature kill, Beau's second-creature finish or Crownsguard jurisdiction without a new explicit episode-level decision.
- She cannot gain a capability from implication. Any additional action needs a governing module and a physical, witness and consequence check.
- The fight cannot present her as only a passive obstacle or reduce her combat to the two opening movements above. Those movements are an opening, not a cap.
- The fight cannot turn the source group into spectators. Their actions must remain chosen, legible and consequential.

## Workflow gate

Before Chapter 03's combat pre-check can be treated as complete or a Chapter 03 loadout can be made, the user must decide the intended middle and finish of Mielle's fight at the level of combat role and allowed capabilities.
