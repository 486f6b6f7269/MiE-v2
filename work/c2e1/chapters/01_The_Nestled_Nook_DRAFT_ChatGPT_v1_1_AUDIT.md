## Knowledge and witness control

### Passes

- Mielle does not infer the private causes of Nott's fear, Caleb's protectiveness, Fjord's demeanor, or Yasha's attention.
- The draft keeps Mielle's coin knowledge incomplete and learns the town name through a nearby exchange.
- Mielle's question about admission is based on information she actually hears.

### Failures

- The sentence “The silence after that had shape” uses an abstract metaphor and the prohibited word “shape.” The later paragraph “The room began to move ... toward something less fragile than silence” assigns a settled social result to a conversation that is still provisional.
- Jester's implied sketch of Mielle after Mielle says “No” to being drawn blurs an explicit boundary. The later narration strongly suggests that Jester has drawn Mielle anyway, including the closed eye, without a new invitation or Mielle's awareness.

## Character voice and agency

### Passes

- Jester is direct, curious and generous.
- Fjord gives a useful, limited answer about the coins and later accepts a partial answer about Mielle's lodging.
- Beau's interest in the wooden sword remains blunt and bounded.
- Caleb and Nott retain concealment, exposure and their own limited participation.
- Molly owns the public carnival invitation.
- Yasha remains observant and restrained.

### Failures

- Jester brings food already selected and physically offers the plate before Mielle has accepted help. Mielle's reply may delay taking it, but it no longer determines whether Jester purchases or provides the food. This conflicts with the Chapter 01 plan's protected sequence: offer → Mielle chooses → food arrives or is shared.
- Mielle's ticket acceptance, “One entry. I do not want you paying for things I have not agreed to,” is too contractual and firm for the intended discomfort. The boundary is not forbidden, but the scene needs hesitant, grateful acceptance rather than a negotiation over terms.
- The actual interaction for Caleb and Nott is brief, while the source group's later cross-table conversation is summarized. Mielle receives the enacted exchange with Nott; Jester, Beau and Fjord's continued first-contact lane is reduced to report. This shifts attention from the source group's social bridge and makes the pair's arrival feel rushed.
- Yasha's first exchange remains only a name attribution. Molly does not introduce her, Yasha does not give a greeting, and the table has no short acknowledgement beyond Jester repeating the names. The C2E1 beat is brief but must be present on the page.

## Source adaptation and Delta compliance

- Caleb protects Nott's social space; Nott chooses her own exposure and response.
- Molly gives the public carnival invitation and performs the card-reading lane.

### Failures

- The card-reading beat begins on the page but is not completed as a reading. Molly turns over three cards, says only “You have excellent taste in trouble,” then uses the cards to advertise the carnival. The plan allows an exact reading to vary, but it still requires Molly's reading rather than a one-line marketing turn. Add a short, charming, non-prophetic interpretation that gives Jester a concrete reaction before the invitation.

Caleb and Nott, Fjord and Beau never make an actual individual or practical decision to go before the chapter ends. The chapter contract requires a concrete evening plan without creating a party oath.
