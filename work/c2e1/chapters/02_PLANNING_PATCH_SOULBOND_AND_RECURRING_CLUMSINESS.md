## Permanent rule applied here

### Soulbound bokken recall

Mielle may voluntarily call the Soulbound bokken back to her hand at any time. The recalled item is the same ordinary wooden sword. The effect does not create a duplicate, improve the weapon, create an attack, or erase the fact that staff saw her surrender it.

Because she can call it back, Mielle is not emotionally or tactically troubled by handing the bokken to Yasha at the carnival entrance. She complies because the rule is ordinary, public and reasonable for the venue. The handover remains real from Yasha’s and the crowd’s perspective while Mielle leaves the item with her.

Mielle does not recall the bokken during Chapter 02. No danger, need or social reason calls for it. A Chapter 03 combat-specific Canon Delta amendment must decide whether she calls it back, accepts a normal return from Yasha, or remains without it. That later decision must account for witnesses and the public rule; it cannot treat the weapon as permanently inaccessible merely because Yasha held it.

### Recurring everyday clumsiness

Mielle’s clumsiness is a real, recurring noncombat drawback. It should emerge naturally when ordinary physical conditions give it room: clothing, bags, furniture, meals, doors, crowds, stairs, carrying, unfamiliar tools or attempts to look composed. It can cost her time, cause minor inconvenience, create embarrassment, or make another person pause to help.

Do not make it constant slapstick, a result of the closed left eye, a signal of helplessness, or proof about her fighting ability. The contrast with combat remains deliberate: routine coordination can fail; immediate danger brings exact movement.

Chapter 02 contains one natural instance during the queue and weapon handover. It is a recurrence of the standing drawback, not a unique event that explains it.
