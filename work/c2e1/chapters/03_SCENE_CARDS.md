## 01. The song and the break

### Present

Mielle, source group, Molly, Yasha, Toya, Gustav, Bo, Kylre, old man, civilians and Crownsguard.

### Board and source obligations

- Mielle begins at outer front-left; source group is front-center; old man is farther inward in the left-front sector.
- Toya sings; old man transforms; evacuation begins; Kylre removes Toya; Crownsguard are stalled by the crowd.
- Jester opens with magic and a duplicate.
- First creature kills the young woman after she evades once.

### Mielle boundary

She allows a public performance to affect her while it appears harmless. No hostile cue exists before transformation. She cannot save the woman because the creature and victim are inward of her edge position and fleeing bodies occupy the route before the second strike lands.

### Holmes map

- Frame: public song, held audience, known tent layout.
- Anomaly: transformation, then visible panic.
- Model: unknown emergency becomes confirmed danger only at the first attack.
- Unknown: cause, transmission, carnival responsibility.
- Committed action: after the woman's death, leave the outer edge through the gap left by the attack.

### Witness / response

Jester owns the first spell. Gustav, Bo and Kylre own evacuation and performer safety. Nobody can infer that Toya caused the change or that Mielle knew it would happen.

### Outcome

Woman dead; first creature scans for another target; crowd bottleneck active; Mielle has an inward lane.

## 02. The weapon that was elsewhere

### Present

Mielle, first creature, Jester, Yasha, Fjord, Beau, Caleb, Nott, Molly, dead woman, civilians and blocked Crownsguard.

### Board and source obligations

- First creature is inside the front-left danger zone; Mielle approaches through a gap rather than through civilians.
- Yasha distributes weapons. Other characters recover their own action lanes.

### Mielle boundary

Mielle walks inward with empty hands. Mid-approach, the same bokken that Yasha held appears directly in Mielle's hand. She does not use the backpack, another item, a portal or a named power.

When the creature selects her, it must commit a direct mass-and-shoulder line. Mielle moves by minimal distance and strikes its planted outside knee with the bokken. The purpose is balance disruption and route denial, not permanent damage or victory.

### Holmes map

- Frame: one creature, civilian crush, weapons not yet returned.
- Evidence: opened gap, creature's target line, planted leg before lunge.
- Model: becoming the nearest stable target buys time.
- Unknown: hidden reach, anatomy, whether it will change target.
- Committed action: minimal evade and knee interruption.

### Witness / response

Jester may react in alarm; Yasha may see the custody anomaly while working; others may see Mielle's choice. No one learns Soulbond or Mielle's wider inventory.

### Outcome

Mielle has bokken and first creature attention. Weapons are back or returning to their owners. The threat remains alive.

## 03. One narrow arc

### Present

Mielle, both creatures, source group, remaining civilians and Crownsguard blocked by crowd.

### Board and source obligations

- Dead woman rises as second creature.
- Uneven dirt, grass, stone and central-pole routes restrict footwork.
- Jester, Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly retain meaningful action.
- Yasha kills first creature; Beau destroys second.

### Mielle boundary

Mielle keeps both creatures in a limited front arc with footwork, parries and line-breaking strikes. She does not kill, use a barrier, explain the transformation, command the group or turn their actions into a rescue of her.

### Holmes map

- Frame: first creature contained; allies armed; crowd still blocks exit.
- Anomaly: woman becomes second creature.
- Evidence: both creatures prefer nearest moving body; shoulders and planted legs reveal committed lunge direction.
- Model: make their attack lines overlap before either reaches the crowd.
- Unknown: altered anatomy, recovery, splitting routes.
- Test: angle one creature into the other's route, yield ground, leave openings for allies.
- Reply: creatures change target or angle; allies enter by their own choices.

### Canon response

- Jester supports through her own magic.
- Fjord chooses a practical combat line.
- Caleb protects Nott and casts from a viable position.
- Nott finds frightened cover and takes her own shot.
- Molly warns or enters close combat on his own lane.
- Yasha takes the first creature.
- Beau ends the second.

### Outcome

Both threats are down. Mielle has taken no finishing strike. Public evidence, bodies and frightened witnesses remain.

## 04. What the guards saw

### Present

Mielle, source group, Molly, Yasha, Gustav, Bo, Crownsguard, carnival workers and survivors.

### Board and source obligations

- Crowd has mostly drained; bodies, blood, weapons and disturbed ground remain.
- Crownsguard take control and restrict departure from Trostenwald.

### Mielle boundary

Mielle does not flee, fight guards, erase evidence or explain the recall. If ordered, she lowers or surrenders the physical bokken without concern for permanent loss. She may answer only the direct question asked.

### Holmes map

- Frame: fight ended, officials arrive.
- Evidence: weapons drawn, bodies, surviving workers, witnesses, guards' demand for stillness.
- Model: containment and testimony come before explanation.
- Unknown: exact custody and legal conclusion.
- Committed action: comply with direct instruction.

### Witness / response

Guards may know a wooden sword appeared in Mielle's hand only if they or a witness had line of sight. They cannot infer the mechanism, origin, full ability set or motive. Shared danger does not create party membership or settled trust.

### Outcome

Chapter ends under Crownsguard control. Mielle is an observed combat participant, the transformations remain unexplained, and nobody is free to leave Trostenwald.
