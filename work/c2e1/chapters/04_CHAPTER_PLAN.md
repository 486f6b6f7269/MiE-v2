## Chapter contract

### Narrative POV and voice

- Primary POV: close Mielle first-person limited if later drafted.
- Any justified brief external POV: none planned.
- Voice or knowledge constraint that must remain visible: Mielle knows she is under local authority but does not know Imperial law, Crownsguard procedure, legal consequences, local lodging custom or the transformation cause. She does not know how much the group noticed during the fight except what they say or visibly react to.

### Must be true by the end

- Source outcomes preserved: Crownsguard question witnesses, identify names, keep the involved people under investigation, forbid leaving Trostenwald, and hold the carnival/Molly/Gustav/Bo under scrutiny.
- Approved / delta-governed changes: Jester publicly covers Mielle as staying with the Nestled Nook group; Mielle accepts.
- Required deaths, injuries, captures, escapes, losses, custody changes or legal conditions: old man/first creature and young woman/second creature remain the only named dead/destroyed bodies from the incident. No new injury. Crownsguard legal control continues. Yasha's source route to Toya may remove her from the tent before the final escort if the scene covers that full source beat. Molly remains with Gustav and Bo if the chapter reaches the source escort-out endpoint.
- Required relationship / trust / debt / group-status non-results: the cover creates a public association and a practical start to co-presence with the group. It does not create friendship, full trust, party membership, debt, ownership, romance, continuing financial support, or permission to demand Mielle's secrets.
- Information that must remain unknown: Mielle's real shelter, origin, powers, Soulbond, Haki, Bag contents, memory archives, power ceiling, closed-eye cause, and the transformation cause.

### Required causal beats

1. Crownsguard and the Watchmaster settle the tent into an official aftermath: weapons down, bodies visible, frightened witnesses, carnival staff under pressure.
2. The Watchmaster asks what happened, who saw it, who belongs to the carnival, and names from the people who helped.
3. The source group keeps a basic united witness account: they saw the transformation, helped stop the creatures, and do not know the cause.
4. Yasha may name Toya as a possible answer and be sent with Flynn if this chapter carries the full source end beat.
5. The Watchmaster asks where the helped group is staying.
6. Fjord owns the practical source answer for the group: the Nestled Nook.
7. Mielle is then included in the lodging accountability. She hesitates because a truthful answer would expose the abandoned shelter and because she is not with the group yet.
8. Jester notices first and cuts in with the cover: Mielle is with them / staying at the Nestled Nook.
9. The rest of the group does not contradict Jester in front of the Watchmaster. Their reactions remain character-specific and do not become a full group oath.
10. Mielle accepts the cover without giving the real shelter or inventing a detailed false biography.
11. The Watchmaster treats Mielle as locatable through the Nestled Nook group and renews the no-leaving order.
12. If the chapter reaches the source endpoint, guards escort the helped group plus Mielle toward town while Molly remains held with Gustav and Bo; exact arrival at the Nestled Nook is not required in this chapter.

## External objective

Crownsguard objective: freeze the scene, identify who belongs to the carnival, identify outside witnesses/combatants, keep everyone locatable and prevent flight before the investigation.

Mielle's immediate objective: avoid making the legal situation worse, avoid revealing her shelter, avoid explaining powers, and survive a public accounting she does not understand.

## Pressure and change

The danger changes from creature violence to official accounting. Mielle is not physically attacked, but she is cornered by a simple bureaucratic question. Her real answer is unsafe; a lie of her own would be brittle because she lacks local context; silence looks suspicious. Jester's cover changes the board by giving the Crownsguard a usable answer and giving Mielle a public association she did not have before.
