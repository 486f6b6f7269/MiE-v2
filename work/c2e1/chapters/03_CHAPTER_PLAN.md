## Chapter contract

### Narrative POV and voice

- Primary POV: close Mielle first-person limited.
- Any justified brief external POV: none planned. Witness reactions must remain visible to Mielle through speech, movement and line of sight.
- Voice and knowledge constraint: Mielle knows only what the public event shows. She does not know the cause of Toya's song, why the old man transforms, why the first attack changes the woman, or whether the transformations can spread again.

### Must be true by the end

- Toya performs; the old man transforms; the public show becomes a civilian emergency.
- The young woman dies from the first creature's second attack and later rises as the second creature. Mielle does not save her.
- Jester makes the source opening magical attack and duplicate action.
- Yasha distributes the source group's weapons before taking her later greatsword lane.
- Mielle walks toward the creatures empty-handed after the woman's death; her bokken appears directly in her hand during the approach.
- The first creature underestimates Mielle because she looks small, soft, half-blind to outsiders, and carries a wooden practice sword.
- Mielle's first strike is an ordinary unreinforced knee test that reveals the creature is harder than a normal human.
- Mielle receives no hits. Her evasiveness comes from exceptional martial skill plus Observation Haki's short combat prediction.
- After the first hardness test, Mielle uses Armament Haki on counterstrikes through the bokken.
- Mielle breaks one arm and one leg of each creature. At least one major limb break must arise during the cooperative phase after allies act.
- Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly each keep meaningful concrete actions in the fight.
- Yasha kills the first creature. Beau destroys the second.
- Crownsguard claim scene control, treat the public deaths as an investigation and keep the carnival and involved people in Trostenwald.
- No one learns Soulbond, Haki, Mielle's origin, full inventory, full combat capacity or the transformation cause.
- Shared danger creates no party membership, debt, permanent trust, ownership, romance or command hierarchy.

### Required causal beats

1. Audience settles into Toya's song. Mielle does not reject it because it is a public performance and no hostile condition is visible.
2. Old man rises from inward left-front area and transforms. Public motion breaks; Toya is extracted through the performers' side.
3. Jester attacks first and creates a duplicate. The first creature turns toward the young woman; she evades once and dies under the second attack.
4. Mielle's non-rescue is caused by inward distance, crowd collapse and timing, not fear, indifference, lost power or weakness.
5. Mielle walks inward through the gap the attack made, empty-handed and not running through civilians.
6. The same physical bokken appears directly in Mielle's hand. Yasha's prior custody makes this a public anomaly where seen.
7. First creature reads Mielle as an easy next target. Mielle avoids by Observation-assisted footwork and strikes its planted knee without Armament.
8. The failed ordinary damage test proves abnormal hardness. Mielle switches later counters to Armament.
9. Yasha returns weapons quickly; group lanes begin forming.
10. Young woman rises as second creature. Mielle keeps both in front of her, receives no hits, breaks limbs through real openings, and stays an active co-fighter.
11. The group acts materially: Jester support/pressure, Fjord route/angle, Caleb protected spell line, Nott cover/shot, Molly civilian/blade lane, Yasha greatsword lane, Beau physical finish.
12. Yasha kills first creature. Beau destroys second.
13. Crownsguard arrive after the threats fall, order weapons down, control witnesses and restrict departure.

## Starting state

Mielle sits at the outer front-left edge of the packed audience. Her physical bokken is in Yasha's public custody with other surrendered weapons, but Mielle can recall it through Soulbond. Jester, Fjord, Beau, Caleb and Nott occupy front-center positions. Molly works within the carnival; Yasha is near the group and weapons. The tent has an entrance flap behind the audience, a performers' flap across the tent, a central support, lanterns, uneven ground and a crowd with no clear safe route once panic begins.

## External objective

At the beginning, Mielle has no combat objective. Once the first woman is dead, her objective becomes local and narrow: stop either creature from reaching another fleeing civilian while preserving space for the other armed people already acting around her.

## Pressure and change

The chapter starts with a performance Mielle has no reason to reject. Its abrupt transformation creates a danger she cannot classify before it happens. The first woman's death changes Mielle's question from whether the scene is strange to which human line the creature can reach next.

The crowd is not background. It blocks Mielle's path to the first victim, slows Crownsguard access, limits caster and weapon lanes, and makes reckless movement itself dangerous. Mielle chooses to become the nearest readable target after the first death. That works only as an opening move; once the second creature rises and the weapons return, the fight becomes cooperative rather than a solo holding pattern.
