## Chapter contract

### Narrative POV and voice

- Primary POV: close Mielle first-person limited.
- Any justified brief external POV: none planned. Witness reactions must remain visible to Mielle through speech, movement and line of sight.
- Voice and knowledge constraint: Mielle knows only what the public event shows. She does not know the cause of Toya's song, why the old man transforms, why the first attack changes the woman, or whether the transformations can spread again.

### Must be true by the end

- Toya performs; the old man transforms; the public show becomes a civilian emergency.
- The young woman dies from the first creature's second attack and later rises as the second creature. Mielle does not save her.
- Jester makes the source opening magical attack and duplicate action.
- Yasha distributes the source group's weapons before taking her later greatsword lane.
- Mielle walks toward the creatures empty-handed after the woman's death; her bokken appears directly in her hand during the approach.
- Mielle contains both creatures through distance, footwork and limited bokken work after the second transforms. She does not kill or decisively end either creature.
- Yasha kills the first creature. Beau destroys the second.
- Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly each keep meaningful actions in the fight.
- Crownsguard claim scene control, treat the public deaths as an investigation and keep the carnival and involved people in Trostenwald.
- The public witnesses a limited but inexplicable part of Mielle's ability. No one learns the mechanism, full limits, origin or full inventory.

### Required causal beats

1. The audience settles into Toya's song. Mielle does not actively reject its emotional effect because it is presented as a performance and no hostile condition is yet visible.
2. The old man rises from the inward left-front row and transforms. The public breaks into motion; Toya is extracted through the performers' side.
3. Jester attacks first. The first creature turns toward the young woman. She evades once, falls, and dies under the second attack.
4. Mielle recognizes the completed attack as real violence. Her surprise is brief and specific: the transformation had no readable hostile precursor, and the woman died inward of Mielle's outer-edge position through a crowd-blocked line.
5. Mielle begins a controlled walk toward the creature without a weapon in hand. She neither races through civilians nor issues evacuation commands.
6. The bokken appears directly in Mielle's hand before she reaches the first creature. Yasha's earlier visible custody becomes a witnessed anomaly.
7. The first creature mistakes Mielle for a smaller, exposed target. She avoids its committed line by minimal distance and strikes its planted outside knee with the bokken to break its immediate balance and civilian route.
8. Yasha distributes weapons. Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly take their own lanes. The crowd remains an active bottleneck.
9. The dead woman rises as the second creature. Mielle does not know why. She uses footwork and narrow strikes to hold both creatures in one front arc while the group enters with recovered weapons.
10. Yasha ends the first creature. Beau ends the second. Mielle does not claim either conclusion.
11. Crownsguard push through after the immediate danger and take control. Mielle does not evade, explain the recall, or claim authority over the investigation.

## Starting state

Mielle sits at the outer front-left edge of the packed audience. Her physical bokken is in Yasha's public custody with other surrendered weapons, but Mielle can recall it through Soulbond. Jester, Fjord, Beau, Caleb and Nott occupy front-center positions. Molly works within the carnival; Yasha is near the group and weapons. The tent has an entrance flap behind the audience, a performers' flap across the tent, a central support, lanterns, uneven ground and a crowd with no clear safe route once panic begins.

## External objective

At the beginning, Mielle has no combat objective. Once the first woman is dead, her objective becomes local and narrow: stop either creature from reaching another fleeing civilian while preserving an opening for the people already acting around her.

## Pressure and change

The chapter starts with a performance Mielle has no reason to reject. Its abrupt transformation creates a danger she cannot classify before it happens. The first woman's death changes Mielle's question from whether the scene is strange to which human line the creature can reach next.

The crowd is not background. It blocks Mielle's path to the first victim, slows Crownsguard access, limits caster and weapon lanes, and makes reckless movement itself dangerous. Mielle chooses to become the creatures' nearest readable route after the first death. That works only until the second creature rises, turning one containment line into a two-body problem.

## Power and threat review

- Meaningful capability, item, inference or defense: direct Soulbond recall of the same bokken; trained martial footwork and bokken work; Holmes-bounded reading of committed motion. Mielle does not use Haki, Spiral, Stand, Bag portal, mental effect or undefined defense.
- Relevant source mechanic or setting constraint: Soulbond recall has no stated travel time but is visible. Mielle may not invent the transformed bodies' anatomy or mechanism. The public tent, civilian bottleneck and Crownsguard jurisdiction restrict action and aftermath.
- Toya's song: Mielle allows the public performance to affect her emotionally. No defensive power is activated because no hostile condition is known. This does not grant knowledge of the song's source or intent.
- Combat pre-check required: yes. The chapter has two hostile transformations, civilian death, crowd terrain, visible unusual gear recall, multiple active allies, source-required kills and legal aftermath.
