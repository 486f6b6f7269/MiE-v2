## Scene 2 — Where were you staying?

### Present characters

Mielle, Jester, Fjord, Beau, Caleb and Nott. Escort guards leave before the question. Unrelated inn staff are absent from the exchange and do not witness it.

### Prose-scale board

- Jester remains near Mielle.
- Fjord and Beau retain separate standing space.
- Caleb and Nott remain close to the route upstairs.

### External purpose

Let the false public address encounter the actual physical condition of Mielle's last two days.

### Immediate pressure

Jester asks the question other people might soften or postpone: where Mielle has actually been staying. Her tone has curiosity and concern, not suspicion. Mielle's first response is bodily embarrassment before language. Because Jester is genuine and Mielle already wants her friendship, Mielle chooses honesty she would probably deny a colder question.

### Holmes scene map

- Test or withholding method: Mielle considers an evasive cover, gives the bounded shelter fact and withholds exact location/origin.
- Smallest committed action: say `an abandoned building` and `two days` without expanding biography.
- Expected reply or falsifying condition: Jester responds to the concrete inadequacy rather than exploiting the exact location; others either narrow practical risk, react once or remain quiet.
- Revised model: if questions become a coordinated extraction chain, Mielle should narrow further; if Jester acts on immediate care without demanding more, the present-intent model strengthens but does not become trust certainty.
- Agency owner after Mielle's intervention: Jester owns the next offer; Fjord/Beau/Caleb/Nott own only their separate immediate reactions.
