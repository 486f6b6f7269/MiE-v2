A public carnival show becomes a civilian emergency. An old man transforms in the inward left-front sector, kills a nearby young woman after Jester's opening attack, and the woman later becomes a second creature. The entrance clogs with fleeing civilians. The source group begins disarmed; Yasha holds their weapons. Crownsguard cannot initially reach the fight.

## Objectives

1. Preserve source first death and second transformation without inventing a loss of Mielle's capability.
2. Prevent further nearby civilian strikes after the woman dies.
3. Give Yasha time to distribute weapons.

5. Leave the fight with public evidence and Crownsguard authority intact.

## Enemy combat language

- Both creatures use direct, brutal, short-range pursuit: shoulders and body mass commit toward the nearest reachable person; hands, jaws and forward momentum seek a simple strike or grab.
- The first creature initially chooses a backing civilian. The second rises into an already crowded field and seeks the nearest viable body.
- Their unknown altered anatomy means Mielle cannot treat them as trained human fighters or assume a permanent injury from a single strike.

## Tactical truth

> The creatures win when panic gives them separate people to reach. Mielle's narrow answer is to become the nearest stable target and keep both attack lines overlapping until armed allies choose their own openings.

## Tactical Holmes map

- Apparent enemy method: direct pursuit of the closest exposed moving person.
- Anomaly, repetition or constraint: first creature's two-strike attack follows a planted forward line; crowd bodies create routes the creature can exploit; second creature adds a new line without explaining the rule.
- Decisive anchor or linked anchors: the creature's shoulder turns before its reach; one leg bears the weight; civilians are moving away behind Mielle; the gap after the woman falls gives Mielle one inward approach.
- Working model: if Mielle occupies the clear lane and gives only measured ground, both creatures will prefer her over the crowd and their routes can be held in a shared arc.
- Remaining alternative: a creature may split wide, recover balance quickly, change target, possess unusual reach or react unpredictably.
- Predictive test, bait or position choice: approach without sprinting, permit first creature to choose Mielle, evade only after commitment, use a knee interruption, then keep both shoulders facing the same pocket of ground.
- Mielle’s action: direct bokken recall, minimal evasion, planted-knee strike, footwork, parries and line-breaking contact. No killing force.
- Enemy reply: balance recovery, changed angle, second creature's emergence, attempts to split around Mielle or reach Molly/Fjord/other civilians.
- Revised model: once two bodies are present, Mielle cannot hold static ground. She must circle around terrain and leave a real entry angle for allies.

## Pressure waves

2. Mielle enters after the death. Bokken recall is public. First creature chooses her; knee interruption denies the immediate civilian route while Yasha distributes weapons.

4. Allies take their chosen lines. Yasha ends first creature; Beau ends second. Crownsguard arrive to evidence, bodies and a witnessed recall anomaly.

## Mielle’s lane and smallest honest response

- Lane: inward front-left containment pocket between creatures and the most immediate fleeing crowd, not the whole tent.
- First answer: do not rush through civilians; approach through the opened gap; recall bokken; evade the committed first lunge by minimal distance; strike the planted outside knee to break the line.
- Sustained answer: use footwork to keep both creatures facing the same 90-degree front arc; deflect or interrupt limbs only when they threaten to split toward civilians; yield ground around the pole/rough floor rather than exchange force.
- Limit: Mielle may not kill, claim a cure, read anatomy perfectly, use an undefined power or remove every civilian risk.
- Visible cost: she is now an obvious combatant, the recall is witnessed, and her narrow pocket can collapse if a creature splits, the crowd surges, or an ally changes the board.

## Escalation trigger

The second creature's rise changes a one-target interception into two-body containment. That is the reason Mielle must use more sustained footwork and bokken control. It is not a reason to reveal a larger power. A further escalation would require a creature reaching a new civilian line or the containment pocket collapsing; this chapter instead resolves through allies' source-owned entries.
