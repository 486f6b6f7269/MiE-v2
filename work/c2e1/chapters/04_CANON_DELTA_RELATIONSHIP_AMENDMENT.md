## Delta decision

During the lodging question, Mielle is included in the public accounting because she is present, visibly involved, and has been standing near the future group after the fight.

When the guard's question reaches her, Mielle visibly hesitates. A truthful answer would expose her secret abandoned shelter, and she does not belong to the group yet. Before she gives a damaging answer, Jester cuts in and says Mielle is with them, staying at the Nestled Nook. Mielle accepts the cover.

This creates the first real public association between Mielle and the group.

## What the relationship change is

- Jester extends a public cover under legal pressure.
- Mielle accepts the cover because it protects her secret shelter and gives the Crownsguard a place to locate her.
- The Crownsguard may now treat Mielle as associated with the Nestled Nook group for the immediate investigation.
- The group has a practical reason to keep Mielle with them after the tent instead of letting her vanish separately.
- This begins real same-evening co-presence and practical contact beyond food, ticket payment and shared combat.

## What the relationship change is not

- Not established friendship.
- Not full trust.
- Not formal party membership.
- Not romance.
- Not debt.
- Not ownership.
- Not ongoing financial support.
- Not a confession of Mielle's real sleeping place.
- Not permission for anyone to demand Mielle's origin, shelter, powers, eye, inventory, memory, or full history.
- Not proof that Mielle has chosen the group's goals or that the group has chosen hers.

## Knowledge effects

### Information created

- Crownsguard can record or assume that Mielle is staying with the Nestled Nook group for the immediate investigation.
- Jester knows Mielle was cornered by the lodging question and accepted the cover.
- Mielle knows Jester publicly covered for her and that the cover was not the truth about her secret shelter.
- Fjord, Beau, Caleb, Nott, Molly and Yasha may know Mielle hesitated and accepted cover only if they had attention and sightline. No universal group knowledge is automatic.
- The public cover gives the group a concrete reason to keep Mielle close after the tent.

### Information withheld

- Mielle's actual shelter location.
- Whether she slept there safely, owns it, stole it, found it, or can return there.
- Her origin, powers, Soulbond, Haki, inventory, memory archives and power ceiling.
- Why she is alone in Trostenwald.
- The cause of the transformations.

### Unsupported conclusions

- The guard cannot know the cover is false unless later evidence shows it.
- The group cannot infer the shelter's location from Mielle's hesitation alone.
- Jester's cover does not prove Mielle trusts Jester.
- Mielle accepting cover does not prove she is committed to travel with the group.

## Mechanics and setting boundaries

- No power is required for the cover beat.
- Mielle does not use Soulbond, Haki, Bag of Holding, Perfect Wardrobe, Gale-Force Reading Glasses, mind effects, or archive knowledge to solve the legal pressure.
- The bokken remains a physical custody issue from Chapter 03. This amendment does not return it, recall it, impound it, or destroy it.
- Mielle's local knowledge remains limited. She does not know local law beyond what the Crownsguard say and what their behavior makes visible.
- Holmes-style observation may let Mielle read that a direct shelter answer is dangerous; it does not create legal expertise or a perfect solution.
- Jester's social impulse supplies the cover. Mielle does not orchestrate Jester into doing it.
