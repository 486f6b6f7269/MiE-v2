## Episode-delta constraints inherited here

- Mielle does not save the young woman. The death occurs before she has a non-collision route through the crowd.
- Mielle walks inward after the completed attack, recalls the same physical bokken directly and visibly, and becomes a local target.
- Mielle receives no hits. This comes from exceptional martial skill plus Observation Haki's short combat prediction, not invulnerability.
- The first knee strike is ordinary and unreinforced. It proves abnormal creature hardness.
- After that first test, Mielle uses Armament Haki on counterstrikes through the bokken.
- Mielle breaks one arm and one leg of each creature through real openings. At least one major limb break happens during the cooperative phase after allies act.
- Mielle does not kill, assign roles, explain powers, explain the threat, claim party status or command the group.
- Direct recall and black-reinforced impacts are public witness facts only where line of sight permits. They do not reveal mechanism.

## Mielle’s allowed intervention

- Allow the performance to affect her emotionally while no hostile condition is known.
- Observe the transformation and first attack without claiming advance knowledge.
- Move inward after the woman dies, using the gap left by the attack rather than driving through fleeing people.
- Recall only her own physical bokken directly to hand.
- Let the first creature select her as an apparently easy target, evade its committed line by minimal distance, and strike the planted outside knee without Armament.
- Use Observation Haki for short warnings of intent, line and timing during hostile exchanges.
- Use Armament Haki on later bokken counterstrikes after abnormal hardness is established.
- Break one arm and one leg of each creature through exposed limbs, committed motion, ally-created pressure or creature instability.
- Create room for others to act without assigning them roles, correcting their choices or claiming their kills.
- Lower or surrender the physical bokken if Crownsguard issue a direct public instruction after immediate danger ends.

## Mielle’s prohibited shortcuts

- No preemptive rescue of the first woman.
- No claim that Toya, the old man, the woman, the song or the carnival caused the threat before evidence permits it.
- No healing, revival, transformation cure, transmission cure, crowd teleportation, mind-reading, crowd control, mass barrier or automatic civilian extraction.
- No recall of anyone else's weapon; no replacement weapon; no duplicate bokken; no projectile use of the recall.
- No advanced Haki emission, internal destruction, Conqueror's Haki, Spiral Power, Stand, Bag portal, mental effect, supernatural barrier, teleportation or undefined protection.
- No physical impossibility presented as footwork. Mielle cannot pass through bodies, occupy two lines at once or reach the first woman through the crowd before the second strike.
- No Mielle finishing kill against either creature. Mielle may impair, contain and create openings, but Yasha and Beau must own the finishes.
- No public explanation of Soulbond, Haki or a system name.
- No party claim, group command, romance, debt, ownership, trust upgrade or relationship conclusion created by shared danger.

## Mechanics and setting constraints

- Soulbond gives recall of the same nonliving bokken at will; no recall travel time is supplied. The direct return is visible.
- Observation Haki gives short combat prediction of immediate hostile intent, line and timing. It is not future sight, mind-reading, explanation or immunity.
- Armament Haki reinforces counterstrikes and can visibly blacken the weapon. It is not advanced emission, internal destruction or a guaranteed bypass of every defense.
- The creatures have unknown transformed anatomy. Mielle may read gross motion and committed weight, not exact weak points or an internal mechanism.
- The tent has uneven grass, dirt and stone, a central pole, a packed crowd, a restricted entrance and a performers' flap that belongs to carnival evacuation rather than civilian escape.
- The song is not treated as hostile before any hostile fact appears. Mielle does not turn on a mental defense against a performance she has chosen to hear.
