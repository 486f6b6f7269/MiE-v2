## Episode-delta constraints inherited here

- Mielle does not save the young woman. The death occurs before she has a non-collision route through the crowd.
- Mielle walks inward after the completed attack, recalls the bokken directly and visibly, and becomes a local target.
- Mielle holds both creatures in a constrained front arc once the second rises. She does not kill, decisively disable or command the group.
- Direct recall is public. It must create witness memory but not a complete explanation.
- No Haki expression, Spiral effect, Stand, portal, mental effect, supernatural barrier or undefined protection is used.

## Mielle’s allowed intervention

- Allow the performance to affect her emotionally while no hostile condition is known.
- Observe the transformation and first attack without claiming advance knowledge.
- Move inward after the woman dies, using the gap left by the attack rather than driving through fleeing people.
- Recall only her own physical bokken directly to hand.
- Let the first creature select her as a target, evade its committed line by minimal distance, and use the bokken against the planted outside knee to disrupt balance and civilian access.
- Maintain a narrow two-creature front arc through footwork, distance, nonlethal line-breaking strikes and controlled retreat around terrain.
- Create room for others to act without assigning them roles, correcting their choices or claiming their kills.
- Lower or surrender the physical bokken if Crownsguard issue a direct public instruction after immediate danger ends.

## Mielle’s prohibited shortcuts

- No preemptive rescue of the first woman.
- No claim that Toya, the old man, the woman, the song or the carnival caused the threat before evidence permits it.
- No healing, revival, transformation cure, transmission cure, crowd teleportation, mind-reading, crowd control, mass barrier or automatic civilian extraction.
- No recall of anyone else's weapon; no replacement weapon; no duplicate bokken; no projectile use of the recall.
- No physical impossibility presented as footwork. Mielle cannot pass through bodies, occupy two lines at once or reach the first woman through the crowd before the second strike.
- No decisive strike against either creature. Mielle may interrupt posture, balance and route, but Yasha and Beau must own the kills.
- No public explanation of Soulbond or a system name.
- No party claim, group command or relationship conclusion created by shared danger.

## Information control

### Information created

- Direct witnesses can see Mielle's bokken appear in hand while it had been in Yasha's custody.
- Direct witnesses can see Mielle choose a creature lane and use exceptional but physically local footwork and bokken work.
- Mielle sees the woman die and later transform, but does not know whether the first creature caused it, whether the rule repeats, or why it happened.
- Crownsguard have additional reason to identify Mielle as a witness and possible combatant.

### Information withheld or still uncertain

- Soulbond's name, range, source, limits and relation to Mielle's other possessions.
- Mielle's origin, broader inventory, maximum ability, memories, shelter or motive for being in Trostenwald.
- Transformation cause, transmission rule, carnival responsibility and official conclusion.
- Whether all civilians saw the recall clearly, and what each remembers through panic.

### Reader-only / author-only facts that must not leak

- Later source explanations, later campaign histories, future relationships and any future factual answer about the creatures.

## Mechanics and setting constraints

- Soulbond gives recall of the same nonliving bokken at will; no recall travel time is supplied. The direct return is visible.
- Mielle's core action uses trained skill, not a new physical enhancement. Bokken work must remain within personal reach and actual footing.
- The creatures have unknown transformed anatomy. Mielle may read gross motion and committed weight, not exact weak points or an internal mechanism.
- A kneestrike can redirect a planted limb and delay a lunge. It cannot be presented as permanent disabling damage or proof that Mielle knows undead anatomy.
- The tent has uneven grass, dirt and stone, a central pole, a packed crowd, a restricted entrance and a performers' flap that belongs to carnival evacuation rather than civilian escape.
- The song is not treated as hostile before any hostile fact appears. Mielle does not turn on a mental defense against a performance she has chosen to hear.
