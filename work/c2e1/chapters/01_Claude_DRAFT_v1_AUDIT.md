- The draft starts inside the food-and-currency problem at the Nestled Nook.
- It keeps Rinaldo's payment separate from Mielle.
- It ends before carnival entry, the performance, transformations, combat or official response.
- Mielle remains a guest with one offered entry, not a party member, debtor, dependent, employee or claimed companion.
- Mielle withholds her shelter, origin, equipment access, powers and private history.

### Failures

- The current active Mielle dossier includes a left eye that is blind and always closed behind the left lens of her glasses. The draft contains neither the eye nor the glasses as a social detail.
- The active C2E1 planning materials were made before this latest user decision and do not list the closed left eye among the visible facts that witnesses may remember. This is a witness-and-knowledge correction, not a decorative prose change.

## Knowledge and witness control

### Passes

- Mielle does not infer the private history, motives or hidden power of the people in the room.
- The text distinguishes what she can see from what she cannot responsibly conclude in several key places, especially Caleb and Nott's guarded arrangement.
- Mielle's sharp social observations are permitted. They are not audit issues merely because they are incisive. The current instruction is to preserve this quality of her narration.

### Required boundary

- Once the closed left eye is written into the scene, direct witnesses may remember only that visible feature and any words Mielle actually gives about it. No character may treat it as proof of her past, emotion, danger, power or intentions.

## Character voice and agency

### Passes

- Jester remains quick, curious and socially direct without becoming a therapist or a mascot.
- Fjord stays practical and restrained.
- Beau remains blunt without becoming a generic bully.
- Caleb and Nott retain a guarded pair dynamic and own their disclosure.
- Molly owns the invitation and card-reading lane. Yasha remains brief and practical.
- Mielle's answer to Nott is appropriately ordinary and does not turn her into an explanation for Nott's exposure.

### Failures

- Jester orders food before Mielle chooses whether to accept it. The line, “I'm not asking if I can pay for it. I'm telling Adelaine what to bring,” turns Mielle's planned accept-or-refuse decision into an action already completed by Jester.
- The opening says that Jester counts her share of Rinaldo's payment. Beau's C2E1 dossier assigns the reward-counting action to Beau. This is a source-character-action error, not a harmless rearrangement.
- The ending leaves Caleb and Nott in a low conversation without committing to the carnival plan. The chapter contract requires the source group and Mielle to have a concrete evening plan, while preserving each person's own reason for attending.

## Source adaptation and Delta compliance

### Passes

- The source structure survives: separate people share the inn, limited contact begins, Molly and Yasha arrive, Molly invites the room, and Mielle accepts a bounded route to attend.
- Mielle does not replace Jester's food offer, Jester's ticket offer, Molly's invitation, Caleb's protection of Nott, or any later combat lane.
- No power, hidden money, storage retrieval or special admission bypass appears.

### Failure

- Molly announces a card reading “free of charge.” His active C2E1 dossier defines the reading as low-cost, and the chapter plan preserves a paid card-reading beat as flexible scene material. The draft must use a modest stated cost or otherwise follow the active source artifact; it cannot silently make the reading free.

- Mielle's coins remain unknown in value; food and carnival admission come from visible, ordinary social choices.
- The wooden sword is not treated as a weapon reveal, power display or carnival shortcut.

## Combat and powers

## Prose and POV

### Passes

- First-person Mielle POV remains intact.
- The opening begins with a concrete, current problem.
- The chapter has clear social movement: hunger becomes an offered meal; strangers become a limited shared room; the carnival becomes a specific evening plan.
- Dialogue is differentiated across Jester, Fjord, Beau, Caleb, Nott, Molly and Yasha.
- The draft uses physical details and interruptions rather than recapping the episode.
- Mielle's close observational style should remain during revision.

### Failure

- The final paragraph explains the chapter's emotional result after the food, coins, shelter and return time have already shown it: “Not belonging, not yet. Just more than I'd had...” This is explanatory aftertaste. End on the concrete remainder instead.
