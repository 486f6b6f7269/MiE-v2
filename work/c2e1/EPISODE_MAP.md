## Episode contract

### Source outcomes that must survive

- The future Mighty Nein begin as separate, only partly compatible people at the Nestled Nook.
- Rinaldo pays Fjord, Jester and Beau for their earlier rescue.
- The group crosses the social gap enough to accept Molly's carnival invitation.
- Molly and Yasha remain the carnival workers who create that invitation and host the setting.
- The group chooses to attend the show.
- Two audience members transform into hostile creatures during the performance.
- The group stops the threats through meaningful ensemble action.
- Crownsguard intervention keeps the carnival and the people involved in Trostenwald under investigation.

### Major pressure changes

1. **Separate survival problems become a shared room.** Caleb and Nott need concealment; Fjord, Jester and Beau have a paid local obligation; Molly and Yasha need an audience; Mielle needs food and a way to read unknown money.
2. **Curiosity makes limited contact possible.** People can recognize visible facts without receiving full biographies or trust. Mielle stays near the convergence, but does not become the reason it happens.
3. **A carnival creates a temporary common destination.** Molly's invitation reaches the whole room. Jester's offer to pay Mielle's entry gives her a practical path to attend without making Mielle a group possession.
4. **A public show turns violent.** Civilian exposure, two hostile transformations, and Mielle's presence require a later combat-specific Delta review. No one gets a free answer merely because Mielle is there.
5. **The town claims jurisdiction.** The fight ends, but the episode's practical consequence is restricted movement, witnesses and an investigation.

### Information movement

- Public at the inn: visible identity, immediate behavior, the payment to Fjord/Jester/Beau, the carnival's existence, and whether someone appears hungry, armed, concealed or cautious.
- Private at the inn: personal history, reasons for travel, hidden power sources, crimes, trauma, affiliations and motives beyond observable conduct.
- Mielle may give surface facts through actual dialogue: her name, that she is unfamiliar with the town's money, that she carries a wooden practice sword, or another equally bounded fact. These do not explain why she is alone or where she came from.
- Public at the carnival: entry procedure, weapon collection, transformations, fighting, immediate casualties and guard response.
- Still unresolved after the episode: the cause and meaning of the transformations, the authorities' final conclusion, and the deeper histories of the group.
- Mielle-specific boundary: her arrival, sealed past, equipment storage, power ceiling and emotional history remain non-public unless actual scene evidence gives a character a narrower fact.

## Setting and material use

- The Nestled Nook is food, lodging, a job board and a public common room in a working town. It is not a fixed floor plan. Scene work decides only what it needs.
- Trostenwald's Imperial authority makes public violence and suspicious deaths a legal problem, not an adventure cue without aftermath.
- Mielle's coins establish uncertainty, not usable purchasing power. A meal price, local courtesy and the social meaning of payment must come from a visible exchange.
- Jester's money comes from the source payment; her offer covers a specific carnival entry rather than giving Mielle a purse, allowance or financial dependency.
- The carnival has workers, tickets, animals, performances, weapons procedure, equipment and a relationship with town authority. It cannot be written as a neutral backdrop.

## Mielle insertion windows

- Window: opening inn through Molly and Yasha's invitation.
  - What source pressure exists: Two separate groups are managing safety, payment and discretion in a public room. Curiosity begins to bridge the tables; the carnival workers arrive with an offer.
  - What she may affect: Jester's attention to hunger; bounded conversation about visible facts; Mielle's dry humor, awkward self-sufficiency and restrained curiosity; low-stakes reactions from Fjord, Beau, Caleb and Nott; the practical question of admission after Molly's invitation.
  - What must not be decided here: whether Mielle joins either group, whether the others have solved her, whether Mielle displaces Jester's contact with Nott, or whether one morning becomes lasting trust.

- Window: carnival entry and performance setup.
  - What source pressure exists: the source group independently comes to the show; Yasha works the entry; the carnival controls weapons and admission.
  - What she may affect: Mielle may accept Jester's paid ticket, comply with the same entry rules, and choose where to stand or sit within the scene's honest staging.
  - What must not be decided here: a special exemption, a hidden payment source, special treatment from the carnival, or a social status Mielle has not earned.

- Window: carnival performance and aftermath.
  - What source pressure exists: a public double threat, civilian danger and official investigation.
  - What she may affect: her immediate local response to danger, subject to later combat review.
  - What must not be decided here: a power answer, a shortcut around the weapons procedure, combat outcome, guard response or a reason the source result remains the same without evidence.

## Later-dependent consequences

- Trostenwald remains a forced stop rather than a town the group can leave after the show.
- The public fight and guard investigation have witnesses and evidence; later scenes must not act as though the episode was private.
- Mielle is present at the carnival and therefore cannot be treated as unaware of its public violence or its official aftermath.
- Early proximity remains limited. A meal, a shared invitation, a paid ticket or a pleasant exchange does not establish loyalty, confession, debt, ownership, romance or party membership.
- Mielle's first food offer, conversation, invitation acceptance and carnival presence are recorded only after approved prose fixes their actual witnesses and words.
