# Party memory and curiosities

## Purpose

This file tracks what people who have met Mielle can reasonably remember, infer, wonder about, ask, and receive as an answer regarding her.

It is a social-interaction controller. It does not replace:

- `continuity/KNOWLEDGE_LEDGER.md` for atomic facts, belief states, mistaken conclusions and sources;
- character dossiers and character states for voice, motive and current position;
- `canon/relationships/RELATIONSHIP_STATE.md` for active relational change;
- event tiles for objective events;
- approved prose for exact wording and scene texture.

## Operating rule

Before a meaningful Mielle/group interaction, classify the relevant material.

| Category | Meaning | Use in prose |
|---|---|---|
| Witnessed fact | A character directly saw or heard it, or was plainly told on-page. | They may remember it, mention it, or act on it. |
| Bounded impression | A reasonable conclusion from more than one witnessed fact. | It may guide a gentle question or reaction, never become proof of origin, motive or hidden mechanism. |
| Open curiosity | A real unanswered question. | It may arise naturally once, then stay quiet until a meaningful new trigger appears. |
| Partial answer | Mielle answered part of a question. | Preserve both the answer and the remaining gap. Do not upgrade it into complete disclosure. |
| Private / no-entry zone | A visible subject not socially available without invitation. | Do not press, repeatedly tease, diagnose, or use it as group entertainment. |

## Non-amnesia rule

Do not write a character as though they have never seen an established fact. A later scene may react to a new scale, cost, boundary or context, but not rediscover the basic witnessed fact.

## Non-omniscience rule

Do not allow a person to convert Mielle’s unusual competence, a visible item, a deduction, a habit, silence, physical limitation, or protective act into confirmed origin, motive, system mechanics, diagnosis, relationship claim, unlimited capability or social permission.

## Social-memory rule

A fact can be socially meaningful without becoming group property. Record who saw it. One person may know a detail through closeness while another has only a distant impression.
