# C2E1-C03_THE_CURIOUS_SHOW — Carnival attack and official lockdown

## Objective story fact

Inside the Fletching and Moondrop carnival tent, Toya sings. During the performance, an old man rises from the inward left-front audience area and transforms. Gustav, Bo and carnival workers begin moving people; Toya is removed through the performers' route. Jester makes the first magical attack and creates a duplicate. The first creature attacks a young woman who is blocked by the crowd; she evades once, then dies before Mielle can reach her through any non-collision route.

After the young woman dies, Mielle walks from the front-left edge into the cleared danger pocket with empty hands. The physical wooden bokken leaves Yasha's custody and appears in Mielle's hand. Mielle draws the first creature's attention, evades its attacks without being hit, tests its hardness with an ordinary knee strike, then uses black-reinforced bokken counters after learning it is harder than a normal human body. The dead young woman rises as the second creature. Mielle keeps both creatures in front of her, breaks one arm and one leg of each creature, and does not kill either one.

Fjord recovers his falchion and takes a flank/route-control angle. Caleb casts from near the pole while keeping Nott close. Nott fires from cover. Molly moves civilians and then uses blades to keep a creature off people behind him. Yasha kills the first creature with her greatsword. Beau destroys the second creature. Crownsguard enter after the immediate danger, order weapons down, control witnesses, count the dead and declare that nobody involved leaves Trostenwald until the Crown allows it. Mielle sets the bokken flat on the dirt and lifts an empty hand. Jester comes to Mielle's left afterward, asks one small question and stays there quietly.

## Direct witnesses

- Mielle: experiences the full chapter. She sees the first transformation, the young woman's death, the second transformation, her own public recall, her own no-hit fight, the group contributions, Yasha's kill, Beau's finish, Crownsguard control and Jester's quiet post-fight presence.
- Jester: sees enough to know Mielle walked at the creature with empty hands and then had the wooden sword; she directly notes Mielle's closed left eye after the fight and does not press the question. Her exact combat sightline may be partial during active chaos.
- Yasha: is at the weapons and sees the wooden sword leave her custody and appear with Mielle. She later kills the first creature and surrenders or lowers her greatsword under Crownsguard order.
- Fjord: sees Mielle fight if his line permits during weapon recovery and melee entry; he contributes a practical angle and later lowers his falchion.
- Beau: enters the fight physically and destroys the second creature; she can know Mielle had impaired the creatures if her line permits.
- Caleb: sees the public danger, protects Nott's position, casts, and may see Mielle's black-edged impacts or recall only if line of sight permits.
- Nott: experiences the danger from cover near Caleb, fires at least one meaningful shot, and may know only fragments of Mielle's movement through fear and obstruction.
- Molly: handles carnival/civilian pressure and close-combat lane; he can know Mielle defended a sector but not the full mechanics.
- Gustav, Bo, Kylre and carnival workers: know the performance collapsed into violence, Toya was removed, civilians were evacuated, and Crownsguard seized the aftermath. Their exact view of Mielle is partial.
- Crownsguard sergeant and guards: see bodies, armed participants, witnesses, Mielle with or near a wooden sword, and weapons being lowered. They do not enter early enough to know the full combat sequence.
- Civilians: have fragmented panic memories. Some may remember a red-cloaked girl with a wooden sword; no universal crowd consensus is established.

## What each witness can reasonably conclude

- Mielle: she could not save the young woman by any route that existed; the creatures are harder than ordinary bodies; her Observation and Armament helped her survive and damage them; the cause of transformation remains unknown; Crownsguard authority is now the immediate constraint.
- Jester: Mielle deliberately walked toward the creature and used herself to draw danger from the crowd. Jester cannot know Soulbond, Haki, Mielle's origin, the limits of her combat skill or why the left eye is closed.
- Yasha: the wooden sword left physical custody and returned to Mielle somehow. Yasha cannot know the name, range, source or limits of Soulbond.
- Fjord, Beau, Caleb, Nott and Molly: Mielle is more dangerous and stranger than first impression suggested if they saw enough. They cannot know source mechanics, power ceiling, origin, memories, full intent or future loyalty.
- Crownsguard: there are two transformed dead/destroyed bodies, armed strangers, carnival workers and a witness problem. They may not treat Mielle's wooden sword as the primary visible lethal weapon unless later questioning or witness statements push that issue.
- Civilians: immediate panic and obstructed sightlines limit reliable memory.

## Unsupported conclusions

- No one knows why the old man transformed, why the young woman transformed, whether Toya caused anything, or whether the first attack transmitted a condition.
- No one can infer romance, party membership, permanent trust, debt, command hierarchy or ownership from Mielle and Jester standing together after the fight.
- No one can infer that the closed left eye caused Mielle's movement, explains her power, or proves her emotion.

## Recallable details

- Toya's voice fills the tent before danger begins.
- Mielle lets the song reach her and does not treat it as hostile before evidence appears.
- The old man rises inward and left of Mielle, closer to the center pole.
- The young woman ducks the first blow but cannot rise before the second.
- Mielle recalls the bokken publicly from Yasha's custody.
- The first ordinary knee strike reveals abnormal hardness but does not break the limb.
- Later counters blacken or darken the wooden edge and break limbs.
- Mielle receives no hit.
- Fjord, Caleb, Nott and Molly make material contributions before the end.
- Yasha kills the first creature; Beau destroys the second.
- The old man and young woman are the only named dead/destroyed bodies created by the chapter.
- Mielle sets the bokken on the dirt when Crownsguard order weapons down.
- The sergeant focuses on scene control, visible lethal weapons and Yasha's greatsword before any full explanation.
- Jester stands to Mielle's left and does not demand the answer behind the closed eye.

## Immediate consequences

- Mielle is physically uninjured but emotionally marked by witnessing a death she could not stop and by steady hands after the fight.
- The physical bokken is on the dirt at the end of the chapter unless later approved prose establishes custody or recall.
- Crownsguard control the tent and forbid departure from Trostenwald.
- The carnival, source group, Mielle and relevant witnesses remain subject to questioning.
- The transformation cause remains an open question.
- Jester has a small new care/curiosity beat with Mielle, but no relationship status change beyond same-scene attention.
