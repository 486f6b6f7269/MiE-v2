# C2E1-C04_UNDER_INVESTIGATION — Crownsguard questioning and Nestled Nook cover

## Objective story fact

After the fight, Crownsguard take control of the Fletching and Moondrop carnival tent. The Watchmaster receives Flynn's panic account and separates observed fact from unsupported accusation: Flynn saw the transformation happen at the carnival, but did not see the carnival cause it.

Guards divide the tent into carnival workers, witnesses, bodies, weapons and questions. Gustav identifies himself as the person who runs the show. Bo and Molly remain on the carnival side under guard pressure. Yasha identifies Toya by name and leaves with Flynn to bring her back.

The source group and Mielle are questioned as witnesses who helped bring the fiends down. No one knows what caused the transformations. Fjord names the Nestled Nook as the group's lodging. When the Watchmaster asks about Mielle, Jester publicly covers for her, claiming Mielle is with them and staying at the Nestled Nook. Mielle confirms the answer because it gives the Crownsguard a lawful place to find her without revealing her private shelter.

The Watchmaster records Mielle with the Nestled Nook. Before escort, Mielle looks from the bokken to a guard; the guard gives a small nod. Mielle recovers the bokken by ordinary hand, wipes loose dirt with her already dirtied cloak, and slides it into the black cloth belt at her waist. The group is escorted out and ordered not to leave Trostenwald.

## Direct witnesses

- Mielle: experiences the full questioning, the danger of the lodging question, Jester's cover, the Watchmaster's demand for words, the Nestled Nook being written beside her name, the guard's nod, and the ordinary recovery of the bokken.
- Jester: knows she publicly covered Mielle at the Watchmaster's question. She sees Mielle accept the cover and later recover the bokken under guard permission. She does not know Mielle's shelter, origin, Soulbond, Haki, power ceiling or true travel history.
- Fjord: witnesses Jester's cover and Mielle's acceptance. He knows the answer puts Mielle under the Nestled Nook entry for the investigation, but he does not know where Mielle actually slept before.
- Beau: witnesses Jester's cover and Mielle's acceptance. She may suspect the answer is incomplete or false, but has no proof of Mielle's shelter, origin or long-term intent.
- Caleb: witnesses Jester's cover and watches the Watchmaster rather than pressing Mielle. He knows silence avoids bringing more Crownsguard pressure onto the group, but he does not know Mielle's truth.
- Nott: witnesses the cover from near Caleb and reacts quietly. She does not know Mielle's shelter or mechanics.
- Yasha: leaves with Flynn before the Nestled Nook answer. She retains the earlier witnessed fact that the wooden sword left her custody during the fight, but she does not witness the final lodging cover unless later source gives her that information.
- Molly: remains with Gustav and Bo under guard pressure. He sees the group leaving and gestures toward Jester, but remains on the carnival side. He does not know the lodging-cover details unless later told.
- Gustav and Bo: remain under guard pressure. They are separated from the source group's release path and do not gain Mielle's private facts.
- Watchmaster and Crownsguard: know Mielle is recorded with the Nestled Nook, know the group is under restriction, know no one gave a reliable cause, and know the bokken was physically recovered under guard supervision. They do not know power mechanics.

## What each witness can reasonably conclude

- Mielle: the legal question of lodging is dangerous because her actual shelter is private and may be treated as trespass or suspicious. Jester is willing to cover her under official pressure, but the cover is not a formal friendship, party membership, debt, romance, trust or permanent arrangement.
- Jester: Mielle needed help answering the lodging question and accepted the Nestled Nook cover. Jester cannot conclude that Mielle has no other shelter, wants to join permanently, trusts the group, or owes her anything.
- Fjord, Beau, Caleb and Nott: Jester put Mielle under their lodging answer and Mielle accepted. They may treat this as a practical complication for the next scene. They cannot conclude Mielle belongs to the group, has told the truth, or intends to stay beyond the investigation demand.
- Watchmaster and Crownsguard: the group can be found at the Nestled Nook and must not leave Trostenwald. They cannot infer the group's private trust state or Mielle's true origin from the lodging answer.
- Yasha and Molly: retain their own separate pressure lanes from the carnival side and do not automatically know the full lodging-cover exchange.

## Unsupported conclusions

- No one learns Mielle's origin, prior shelter location, Soulbond, Haki, inventory, memory archives, power ceiling or true arrival history.
- Jester's claim that Mielle came from a distant place is cover, not knowledge.
- The Nestled Nook record is not friendship, party membership, legal adoption by the group, debt, romance, ownership, permanent lodging, or consent to disclose Mielle's private facts.
- The group silence is practical non-contradiction under Crownsguard pressure, not settled trust.
- The bokken recovery is ordinary hand retrieval with guard permission, not Soulbond recall and not evidence that the Crownsguard understand its earlier return.
- The Watchmaster's restraint with panic testimony does not solve the transformation cause or clear the carnival.

## Recallable details

- Flynn says he saw the old man change and assumes the carnival did something.
- The Watchmaster asks whether Flynn saw the carnival do it, then records the difference.
- Jester gives her name and adds the Traveler's with public brightness.
- Nott gives `The Brave` as part of her name; the guard writes less than all of it.
- Yasha says `Toya` and leaves with Flynn to bring the girl back.
- The Watchmaster notes the group's help but keeps them under investigation.
- Fjord answers `Nestled Nook` for the group.
- Jester answers for Mielle too quickly and too warmly.
- Mielle says `Yes` after the Watchmaster demands words.
- No one contradicts Jester's cover.
- Molly remains with Gustav and Bo under guard pressure and gives a small finger-bow toward Jester.
- A guard nods permission for Mielle to recover the bokken.
- The Watchmaster's final order is: `Nestled Nook. Until called. No leaving Trostenwald.`

## Immediate consequences

- Mielle leaves the tent physically uninjured, with the bokken at her waist and the legal restriction not to leave Trostenwald.
- Crownsguard records place Mielle at the Nestled Nook for investigation even though she had not slept there before.
- Mielle's prior shelter remains unrevealed.
- Gustav, Bo and Molly remain separated on the carnival side under guard pressure.
- Yasha is away with Flynn to bring Toya back.
- The transformation cause remains unknown.
- The practical meaning of `Mielle is staying with us at the Nestled Nook` is now an immediate social problem for the next chapter.
